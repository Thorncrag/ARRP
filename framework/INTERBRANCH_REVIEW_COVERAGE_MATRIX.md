# Interbranch Review Coverage and Independent Alternatives Matrix

This matrix applies the [General Anti-Nullification Review Framework](INTERBRANCH_REVIEW_FRAMEWORK.md). JUD-011 is generally applicable when its statutory elements are met; no issue or statute opts in. The matrix is a project classification and drafting record, not a legal gateway to JUD-011 coverage.

## Current Automatic Civil Applications

| Issue | Preferred remedy | Independent alternative | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [REG-001](../areas/REG/issues/REG-001.md) | Enact JUD-011 alone | Enact standalone REG-001 alone | Substantial and sustained executive disablement can functionally nullify an enacted institution or mandatory statutory duty. |
| [FUND-001](../areas/FUND/issues/FUND-001.md) | Enact JUD-011 alone | Enact standalone FUND-001 alone | Directive-based withholding, redirection, or program suspension can functionally nullify enacted appropriations and program mandates. |

## Current Express Shared-Infrastructure Applications

These issues do not arise under section 2303. Their preferred Acts use JUD-011's infrastructure only through independent constitutional authority and an express later-Act addition.

## Undeveloped Issues for Later Screening

Undeveloped proposals should not be classified by area. During drafting, screen any issue alleging executive abolition, suspension, redirection, or sustained nonexecution of an enacted institution, appropriation, program, reporting duty, records duty, or other affirmative command. Potentially relevant issue families include specific agency closures, cross-agency operational control, statutory reporting or records obstruction, grant or benefit program suspension, and mandatory oversight-access duties.

The following current candidates foreseeably could benefit, but none should identify JUD-011 as preferred until its own Change Audit establishes every coverage element and a plausible Article III plaintiff:

| Candidate | Question requiring development |
| --- | --- |
| FUND-003 — agency closure or program nullification through nonspending | Does the conduct substantially and sustainably defeat a specific enacted appropriation or mandatory program rather than exercise lawful spending discretion? |
| FUND-004 / RET-001 / FED-003 — selective withholding or coercive funding conditions | Is an enacted payment, grant, contract, or formula command being nullified, and does a concretely injured recipient or State have restorative relief? |
| EMERG-002 — emergency redirection of appropriated funds | Does the emergency action displace a specific enacted funding command without statutory authorization? |
| CIV-009 — cross-agency operational control | Has the instrument substantially disabled a target agency's mandatory statutory function? JUD-011 would not govern the instrument's appointments, privacy, procurement, or delegation defects as such. |
| OVS-003 and related CONG candidates — denial of records, access, testimony, or reporting | Does a statute create a concrete affirmative access or reporting duty, and is there a plaintiff with judicially cognizable injury? Political oversight frustration alone is insufficient. |
| REC-001 — concealment, destruction, or nonpublication of federal records and data | Does the conduct nullify an enacted preservation or publication command, and can prospective relief preserve or restore compliance? |
| [DOM-005](../areas/DOM/issues/DOM-005.md) — Federal critical-incident evidence preservation and State investigative access | JUD-011 does not create the missing evidence-preservation and investigative-access duty. If Congress enacts DOM-005's mandate, does later executive conduct substantially and sustainably nullify it, and does an investigating State have a concrete injury and restorative relief? Is immediate incident-specific relief still required notwithstanding any parallel JUD-011 claim? |
| [APPT-001](../areas/APPT/issues/APPT-001.md) — confirmation and statutory appointment-safeguard evasion | Does temporary service, delegation, removal, or succession conduct substantially and sustainably nullify an enacted office or appointment safeguard rather than present an ordinary FVRA, personnel, or confirmation dispute? Does an injured person have restorative relief, and is APPT-001's subject-specific remedy the better fit? |
| FRB-001 — Federal Reserve removal and continuity | Does removal or succession conduct functionally nullify a statutory institutional mandate, and is the claim not better resolved through the Federal Reserve-specific remedy or REG-001 alternative? |

Screening must answer:

1. What enacted mandate is being enforced?
2. What executive conduct allegedly nullifies it?
3. Why is the effect substantial and sustained rather than an ordinary legal or policy dispute?
4. What lawful discretion remains protected?
5. Who has a concrete Article III injury?
6. What restorative relief can a court order?

If all six questions have adequate answers, JUD-011 already applies by its own terms; no conforming opt-in legislation is required. The issue page may then identify JUD-011 alone as preferred and must develop a separate standalone alternative. Moving a developed issue into or out of this matrix requires a proposal-specific Change Audit. Changing the general test requires a project-level Change Audit.
