# General Anti-Nullification Review Framework

## Objective

[JUD-011](../areas/JUD/issues/JUD-011.md) is ARRP's preferred general remedy when executive action or deliberate inaction substantially and sustainably nullifies an enacted congressional mandate. It creates the civil claim and complete Civil Compliance mechanism itself. Coverage turns on the alleged conduct and statutory mandate, not the policy field, the date of the mandate, or an opt-in designation in another Act.

JUD-011 also creates reusable Article III infrastructure. A later Act supported by independent constitutional authority may expressly add a separately firewalled specialized component that shares the national roster and nondecisional administration. That use is not automatic civil coverage, does not arise under the anti-nullification cause, and may not merge jurisdiction, panels, decisional staff, dockets, protected records, or appellate routes.

The framework addresses the recurring political-enforcement gap that arises when unified party control or other political constraints prevent Congress from defending its own enactments. A rule-bound judicial avenue cannot eliminate Article III limits, but it can make enforcement less dependent on a later congressional majority choosing to act.

## Governing Rule

JUD-011 must:

1. create a direct, generally applicable civil cause of action;
2. apply across subject matters when the statutory anti-nullification test is satisfied;
3. reach continuing or future executive conduct affecting mandates enacted before, on, or after JUD-011;
4. identify eligible plaintiff categories while preserving Article III injury, causation, and redressability;
5. waive sovereign immunity for authorized nonmonetary relief;
6. provide D.D.C. jurisdiction, neutral panel assignment, expedition, interim preservation, fact development, final relief, and D.C. Circuit review;
7. authorize relief that restores compliance with the enacted mandate without transferring lawful policy discretion to the court; and
8. operate without any designation, incorporation, or enactment of a subject-specific proposal.

No ARRP proposal should describe an ordinary civil anti-nullification claim as one that “joins,” “activates,” or “participates in” JUD-011. That civil case is covered because it satisfies JUD-011's elements. A proposal relying on independent constitutional authority may describe an express later-Act addition to JUD-011's shared infrastructure only if the later Act defines the specialized authority and preserves the required firewalls.

## Coverage Test

JUD-011 is the preferred remedy only when all of the following are present:

1. **Enacted mandate.** Federal law establishes an affirmative duty, institution, appropriation, program, limitation, or other legal command.
2. **Executive conduct.** A senior executive officer, agency, or instrumentality acts or deliberately fails to act in a manner affecting that mandate.
3. **Nullifying effect.** The conduct suspends, redirects, evades, or functionally nullifies the mandate.
4. **Substantial and sustained injury.** The effect resembles practical repeal, abolition, or suspension, rather than an isolated error or ordinary implementation dispute.
5. **No lawful discretion.** The claim does not merely contest enforcement priority, policy judgment, temporary administration, or conduct Congress committed to executive discretion.
6. **Restorative relief.** A court can restore compliance with the statutory command without directing discretionary policy choices or issuing an advisory opinion.

Urgency, national importance, political inhibition, fragmented litigation, or possible benefit from specialized judges may strengthen the case for expedition but cannot independently establish coverage.

## Independent-Alternative Requirement

Every issue page that identifies JUD-011 or its infrastructure as part of the preferred remedy must also identify a separate, independently enactable alternative. Two patterns are permitted.

### Automatic civil-coverage pattern

### Primary remedy — JUD-011 alone

JUD-011 supplies the generally applicable claim, eligible-plaintiff rules, coverage threshold, forum, procedure, relief, administration, and funding. The subject-specific proposal is not part of this enactment package.

### Independent alternative — subject-specific bill alone

The alternative bill must fully address the issue without invoking or depending on JUD-011 or any other ARRP proposal. It must supply its own substantive rule, cause of action or enforcement authority, plaintiffs, jurisdiction, procedure, relief, administration, transition, and necessary funding.

This structure preserves Congress's choice to enact JUD-011 once for all qualifying anti-nullification disputes or to reject that general framework and enact only a narrower subject-specific remedy. These are alternative legislative vehicles, not two modes of the same bill.

### Express shared-infrastructure pattern

When a proposal does not arise under section 2303 but has independent constitutional authority for a specialized judicial function, the issue page may present:

1. **Preferred:** enact JUD-011 and the issue-specific shared-framework implementing Act, together with any constitutional amendment common to both paths.
2. **Independent alternative:** enact the standalone issue-specific Act, together with the same common constitutional amendment if required.

The preferred implementing Act must be substantively complete, must expressly add the specialized component, and must define its authority, panel assignment, decisional personnel, protected records, review, incremental funding, and relationship to Civil Compliance proceedings. The standalone alternative must operate without JUD-011 and must supply all duplicated infrastructure itself.

If Congress enacts both, each law operates according to its own terms. Legislative counsel should address overlapping claims, consolidation, and remedy coordination without making either Act a prerequisite to the other.

## Existing and Future Mandates

JUD-011's cause of action applies to covered executive conduct occurring or continuing after its effective date, whether the underlying mandate was enacted before, on, or after JUD-011. This is not retroactive liability for completed conduct. It is present enforcement of an existing legal command against ongoing or future nullification.

A later subject-specific statute may supplement JUD-011 with a narrower substantive rule or remedy. JUD-011 remains available unless Congress expressly makes the later remedy exclusive or expressly displaces JUD-011 for the covered claim.

## Initial Application and Boundary

- [REG-001](../areas/REG/issues/REG-001.md): JUD-011 alone is the preferred general remedy; the standalone REG-001 bill is the independent alternative.
- [FUND-001](../areas/FUND/issues/FUND-001.md): JUD-011 alone is the preferred general remedy; the standalone FUND-001 bill is the independent appropriations-specific alternative.
- [DOJ-007](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-007.md): an express shared-infrastructure application after ratification of its constitutional amendment. Its preferred Act adds a firewalled Investigative Counsel component using JUD-011's roster and administration; its standalone Act is the independent alternative.
- [JUD-001](../areas/JUD/issues/JUD-001.md): outside the framework because enforcement of an existing court order is a different institutional failure.
- ELEC-001, ELEC-006, ELEC-012, and EMOL-015: outside the framework on their present issue theories; urgency or political sensitivity does not establish anti-nullification coverage.

The [Coverage and Independent Alternatives Matrix](INTERBRANCH_REVIEW_COVERAGE_MATRIX.md) records current applications, exclusions, and undeveloped issues that warrant later screening.

## Issue-Page Presentation

For each approved automatic civil-coverage application, the Issue Snapshot and **Proposed Legislation** section must state the congressional choice directly:

1. **Preferred:** enact JUD-011 alone.
2. **Independent alternative:** enact the named standalone issue bill alone.

An **Interbranch Review Pathways** section may explain why the issue satisfies the general coverage test and how the standalone alternative differs. It must not describe a two-bill preferred package, an opt-in, a fallback mode, or a later transition from one bill into the other.

For an approved express shared-infrastructure application, the page must instead identify the common constitutional predicate, the JUD-011-plus-implementing-Act preferred package, and the fully independent standalone alternative. It must explain that shared infrastructure does not create substantive or investigative authority.

Every affected issue page must contain two separately labeled budgetary impact statements: one for the preferred JUD-011 path and one for the independent alternative. A combined paragraph is insufficient.

## Appropriations and Administration

JUD-011 must carry the judicial startup, panel, clerk, secure-record, technology, reporting, and related administrative costs of the general framework. A later shared-infrastructure Act must identify incremental shared costs and separately fund function-specific operations that JUD-011's base appropriation does not cover. Each independent alternative must carry its own implementation and judicial-administration costs. Cost estimates should distinguish one-time readiness, annual court operations, agency implementation, investigation or litigation-specific expenditures, and avoided duplication and should receive AOUSC, CBO, GAO, appropriations, PAYGO, and legislative-counsel review.

## Review Gates

Before an issue page identifies JUD-011 as the automatic preferred civil remedy, its Change Audit and Internal Remedy-Fit review must confirm all six coverage elements and a plausible Article III plaintiff. Before a specialized shared-infrastructure path is approved, the audit must confirm independent constitutional authority, express later-Act authorization, decisional and records firewalls, ordinary criminal and appellate protections where relevant, and separately identified incremental costs. Before an independent alternative is presented as complete, its audit must verify that no provision depends on JUD-011 and that the bill independently supplies every necessary substantive, procedural, remedial, administrative, and funding component.

Changing JUD-011's general coverage test, adding or removing a developed issue, or revising this two-choice presentation rule requires a project-level Change Audit and updates to this framework, the coverage matrix, affected issue pages, audit sidecars, and authoritative project records.
