# DOJ-007 — Independent Special Prosecutor Appointment and Accountability Act

## Framework Cross-Reference

This proposal implements the constitutional amendment developed under [DOJ-007 — Special-Counsel Dependence and Removal Vulnerability](../areas/a-01-department-of-justice/issues/DOJ-007.md). It is intended to take effect only after ratification of an amendment expressly authorizing judicial initiation and appointment of an independent special prosecutor in cases of extraordinary executive conflict.

## Proposed Bill

### A BILL

To establish procedures for the judicial appointment, authority, independence, accountability, and termination of independent special prosecutors in extraordinary cases of executive conflict, and for other purposes.

**Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,**

## SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the **“Independent Special Prosecutor Appointment and Accountability Act.”**

## SEC. 2. PURPOSES.

The purposes of this Act are—

1. to ensure that credible allegations of federal criminal conduct are not suppressed, distorted, or terminated because the President or senior executive officials control the ordinary prosecutorial chain of command;
2. to provide a narrowly confined procedure for independent investigation and prosecution when an extraordinary conflict materially impairs the reliability of ordinary Department of Justice processes;
3. to provide an alternative constitutional method of appointing a Special Prosecutor outside the executive branch without displacing ordinary executive appointment when no extraordinary conflict exists;
4. to preserve the distinction between judicial appointment and prosecutorial decisionmaking;
5. to protect the independence of an appointed Special Prosecutor while maintaining accountability to law, courts, Congress, professional standards, generally applicable Department of Justice rules and policies, and defined removal procedures;
6. to authorize investigation and preservation of evidence concerning a sitting President when the statutory conditions are satisfied; and
7. to provide a lawful mechanism for transmitting substantial and credible information relevant to the constitutional impeachment function of the House of Representatives without bypassing or replacing impeachment.

## SEC. 3. DEFINITIONS.

In this Act:

### (1) COVERED OFFICIAL.

The term **“covered official”** means—

- the President or Vice President;
- the Attorney General, Deputy Attorney General, or Solicitor General;
- the Director or Deputy Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation;
- the White House Counsel;
- an officer or employee of the Executive Office of the President serving in a position designated by law or regulation as senior or confidential;
- a Cabinet officer;
- an officer of the Department of Justice appointed by the President or serving in an acting capacity in such an office;
- an officer or employee of a presidential campaign, presidential transition, inaugural committee, or national political committee when the alleged conduct materially concerns the President or Vice President; or
- any person whose investigation or prosecution presents a substantial personal, political, financial, familial, evidentiary, or institutional conflict for the President, Attorney General, or Department of Justice leadership.

### (2) CREDIBLE INFORMATION.

The term **“credible information”** means specific and articulable information that, viewed in light of its source, corroboration, and surrounding circumstances, provides a reasonable basis to believe that a federal offense, obstruction, destruction or concealment of evidence, retaliation against a witness or investigator, or unlawful interference with federal law enforcement may have occurred.

### (3) EXTRAORDINARY EXECUTIVE CONFLICT.

The term **“extraordinary executive conflict”** means an actual or objectively reasonable conflict of interest that materially impairs the capacity or perceived capacity of the Department of Justice to investigate or prosecute a matter impartially, including a conflict arising from presidential control, personal interest, prior representation, political loyalty, exposure of Department leadership, or authority over the personnel, evidence, resources, or charging decisions at issue.

### (4) SPECIAL DIVISION.

The term **“Special Division”** means the judicial panel established under section 4.

### (5) SPECIAL PROSECUTOR.

The term **“Special Prosecutor”** means an inferior officer appointed under this Act pursuant to the constitutional amendment authorizing judicial appointment in cases of extraordinary executive conflict.

### (6) DEPARTMENT RULE OR POLICY.

The term **“Department rule or policy”** means a generally applicable internal rule, regulation, manual provision, written policy, charging standard, ethics requirement, or professional practice of the Department of Justice that would ordinarily govern a United States Attorney or Department prosecutor performing comparable work.

## SEC. 4. SPECIAL DIVISION.

### (a) ESTABLISHMENT.

There is established within the judicial branch a **Special Division for Independent Prosecution Appointments**.

### (b) COMPOSITION.

The Special Division shall consist of three judges of the United States courts of appeals selected under a random and publicly prescribed procedure established by the Judicial Conference of the United States, except that—

1. no two judges may be selected from the same circuit;
2. no judge may be selected who has participated in a proceeding materially related to a matter pending before the Special Division;
3. the membership shall reflect staggered four-year terms; and
4. a judge may not serve consecutive terms.

### (c) DISQUALIFICATION.

A judge serving on the Special Division—

1. shall not participate in any criminal, civil, or appellate proceeding arising from an appointment made during that judge’s service;
2. shall not receive confidential investigative information except as strictly necessary to perform the functions assigned by this Act; and
3. shall recuse from any matter in which impartiality might reasonably be questioned.

### (d) LIMITED FUNCTION.

The Special Division may determine whether the statutory conditions for appointment exist, appoint a Special Prosecutor, define or modify jurisdiction, receive notices required by this Act, adjudicate a petition for removal, resolve disputes expressly assigned by this Act, and determine when an appointment has terminated. The Special Division may not direct investigative steps, charging or declination decisions, plea negotiations, trial strategy, sentencing positions, appellate arguments, or departures from Department rules or policies.

## SEC. 5. AUTHORIZED APPLICATIONS AND REFERRALS.

### (a) AUTHORIZED APPLICANTS.

A verified application for appointment of a Special Prosecutor may be submitted under seal by—

1. the Attorney General or Deputy Attorney General;
2. an Inspector General whose statutory jurisdiction materially relates to the alleged conduct;
3. the Director of the Office of Government Ethics;
4. either House of Congress by resolution;
5. the Committee on the Judiciary of either House, by majority vote;
6. not fewer than one-third of the members of the Committee on the Judiciary of either House, provided that the applicants include members affiliated with more than one political party;
7. an Article III court acting under subsection (b); or
8. another officer or institution expressly authorized by Act of Congress.

### (b) JUDICIAL REFERRAL.

An Article III court may transmit a sealed referral to the Special Division when, in a matter properly before that court—

1. the court enters an order or judgment determining that conduct by a covered official or person acting under the authority of a covered official was unlawful;
2. the record contains substantial and credible information that the conduct may also constitute a federal offense, obstruction, destruction or concealment of evidence, retaliation, or unlawful interference with federal law enforcement; and
3. the court finds that an extraordinary executive conflict may materially impair ordinary investigation or prosecution.

### (c) LIMITS ON EFFECT OF JUDICIAL FINDING.

A referral under subsection (b)—

1. is not a finding of probable cause, criminal liability, corrupt intent, or an impeachable offense;
2. is not admissible in a criminal trial to prove any element of an offense;
3. does not bind the Special Prosecutor, a grand jury, or a trial court; and
4. requires the Special Prosecutor to conduct an independent investigation and make independent prosecutorial judgments.

### (d) REFERRING JUDGE.

A judge participating in a referral under subsection (b) may not serve on the Special Division with respect to that matter and may not preside over any resulting criminal case.

### (e) NO PRIVATE RIGHT TO PETITION.

A private person may provide information to an authorized applicant but may not file an application directly with the Special Division.

## SEC. 6. THRESHOLD FOR APPOINTMENT.

### (a) REQUIRED FINDINGS.

The Special Division shall appoint a Special Prosecutor only upon written findings that—

1. credible information indicates that a federal offense or related obstruction may have occurred;
2. the matter concerns a covered official or otherwise presents an extraordinary executive conflict;
3. ordinary Department of Justice procedures, including recusal and internal reassignment, are inadequate to provide reliable and publicly credible investigation or prosecution; and
4. appointment is necessary and proportionate to protect the fair administration of federal criminal law.

### (b) STANDARD OF REVIEW.

The Special Division’s threshold determination shall not decide guilt, probable cause, immunity, admissibility, impeachment, or the merits of any defense.

### (c) WRITTEN ORDER.

An appointment order shall identify—

1. the general subject matter and jurisdiction of the appointment;
2. the factual and institutional basis for finding an extraordinary executive conflict;
3. the powers initially conferred;
4. any reporting or coordination requirements consistent with prosecutorial independence; and
5. the reasons less intrusive measures would be inadequate.

### (d) SEALED AND PUBLIC VERSIONS.

The Special Division shall issue a sealed order and, when consistent with investigative integrity, grand-jury secrecy, classified-information protection, and individual rights, a public version containing the maximum reasonably disclosable information.

### (e) FRIVOLOUS OR ABUSIVE APPLICATIONS.

The Special Division shall dismiss an application that is conclusory, materially unsupported, repetitive without new evidence, or submitted for an improper purpose. The Division may refer knowing false statements or unlawful disclosure of protected information to appropriate authorities.

## SEC. 7. QUALIFICATIONS AND APPOINTMENT.

### (a) QUALIFICATIONS.

A person appointed as Special Prosecutor shall—

1. be a citizen of the United States and a member in good standing of a State or Federal bar;
2. possess substantial experience in federal criminal investigation, prosecution, judicial service, or comparably complex litigation;
3. demonstrate independence, integrity, impartiality, and capacity to manage a complex investigation;
4. have no personal, financial, professional, or political conflict that would materially impair the appointment; and
5. not have represented, been employed by, or served as a senior political adviser to a principal subject of the investigation during the preceding seven years.

### (b) MERIT-BASED SELECTION.

The Special Division shall select the Special Prosecutor through a merit-based process. The Judicial Conference may maintain a confidential roster of qualified candidates, but the Special Division is not limited to that roster.

### (c) OATH AND STATUS.

The Special Prosecutor shall take the oath required of officers of the United States and shall serve as an inferior officer independent of presidential direction as provided by the constitutional amendment and this Act.

### (d) ALTERNATIVE APPOINTMENT ROUTE.

Appointment under this Act is an alternative method of appointing a Special Prosecutor when an extraordinary executive conflict exists. Nothing in this Act prevents the Attorney General or another executive officer from appointing a special counsel or prosecutor under otherwise lawful authority when the requirements of this Act are not invoked.

### (e) TERM.

The Special Prosecutor shall serve until the assigned matter is completed or the appointment is terminated under section 16. Continued service shall be reviewed annually only for jurisdiction, expenditure, progress, and continued necessity, and not for the merits of investigative or prosecutorial decisions.

## SEC. 8. JURISDICTION.

### (a) INITIAL JURISDICTION.

The Special Prosecutor shall have jurisdiction over—

1. the matters identified in the appointment order;
2. federal offenses arising from the same course of conduct;
3. perjury, false statements, obstruction, witness tampering, evidence destruction, retaliation, contempt, and other offenses arising from or intended to interfere with the investigation; and
4. persons who knowingly aid, abet, conspire in, conceal, or obstruct conduct within the assigned jurisdiction.

### (b) MATERIAL EXPANSION.

The Special Prosecutor shall seek approval from the Special Division before materially expanding jurisdiction beyond subsection (a). The request shall describe the proposed expansion without disclosing more investigative detail than necessary.

### (c) NO MERITS REVIEW.

The Special Division shall review a jurisdictional request only to determine whether the proposed matter is sufficiently connected to the appointment and whether expansion remains necessary because of the extraordinary executive conflict.

## SEC. 9. POWERS AND DUTIES.

### (a) GENERAL AUTHORITY.

Within assigned jurisdiction, the Special Prosecutor shall possess the full investigative and prosecutorial authority of the Attorney General and a United States Attorney, including authority to—

1. conduct grand-jury proceedings;
2. issue or seek subpoenas, warrants, immunity orders, and other lawful process;
3. initiate, decline, dismiss, negotiate, try, and appeal federal criminal cases;
4. seek civil or criminal forfeiture related to charged conduct;
5. appoint and supervise attorneys, investigators, analysts, experts, and support personnel;
6. request or direct assistance from federal investigative agencies as provided in section 10; and
7. exercise any additional authority necessary and proper to perform the assigned functions.

### (b) INDEPENDENT JUDGMENT.

No officer or employee of the United States may direct or countermand a particular investigative or prosecutorial decision of the Special Prosecutor.

### (c) DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE RULES AND POLICIES.

1. Except as provided in paragraph (2), the Special Prosecutor shall comply with all Department rules and policies that would ordinarily govern a United States Attorney or Department prosecutor performing comparable work.
2. The Special Prosecutor may depart from a nonstatutory Department rule or policy upon determining in good faith that—
   - compliance would materially impair the independence or integrity of the investigation or prosecution;
   - the rule or policy is affected by the extraordinary executive conflict supporting the appointment;
   - compliance would materially frustrate the appointment order;
   - the rule or policy is inapplicable or materially unsuitable to the circumstances; or
   - another substantial law-enforcement or constitutional interest constitutes good cause.
3. Before departure, or as soon afterward as practicable when advance notice would materially prejudice the investigation, the Special Prosecutor shall submit to the Special Division a sealed written notice identifying the rule or policy, the scope of departure, and the good cause supporting it.
4. Notice under paragraph (3) does not require approval. The Special Division may preserve the notice, determine whether the departure exceeds assigned jurisdiction, and consider a knowing and material abuse of the departure authority in a removal proceeding, but may not approve, disapprove, revise, or countermand the Special Prosecutor’s policy judgment.
5. The Special Prosecutor shall include in the final report a public description of material departures to the maximum extent consistent with grand-jury secrecy, classified-information protection, fair-trial rights, and legitimate privacy interests.
6. Nothing in this subsection authorizes departure from the Constitution, an Act of Congress, the Federal Rules, a binding judicial decision, or an enforceable rule having the force of law.

### (d) INVESTIGATION OF A SITTING PRESIDENT.

1. Notwithstanding any opinion, policy, regulation, practice, or directive of the Department of Justice, the Special Prosecutor may investigate a sitting President for conduct within assigned jurisdiction.
2. The authority under paragraph (1) includes authority to—
   - collect, preserve, analyze, and present evidence;
   - conduct grand-jury proceedings;
   - interview witnesses and subjects;
   - seek subpoenas, warrants, court orders, and other lawful compulsory process;
   - litigate privilege, immunity, classification, and access disputes;
   - investigate obstruction, retaliation, witness tampering, evidence destruction, or interference with the investigation; and
   - transmit substantial and credible information to the House of Representatives as authorized by section 14.
3. No officer or employee of the United States, including the President or Attorney General, may terminate, limit, delay, or interfere with an investigation on the ground that the subject or target is the sitting President.
4. Investigative process directed personally to the President shall be reasonably tailored and scheduled to avoid unnecessary interference with official duties, consistent with the need for timely and effective investigation.
5. Authority to investigate does not by itself determine whether a sitting President may be indicted, tried, convicted, or punished while in office. Those questions shall be governed by the Constitution and any applicable constitutional amendment.
6. Nothing in this Act by itself enlarges or diminishes substantive presidential immunity or the pardon power, except that neither immunity from prosecution nor any Department policy shall be construed as immunity from investigation, evidence preservation, or lawful transmission of information to the House of Representatives.

## SEC. 10. INVESTIGATIVE PERSONNEL, RECORDS, AND AGENCY ASSISTANCE.

### (a) INDEPENDENT CAPACITY.

The Special Prosecutor may employ a minimum independent investigative staff sufficient to prevent effective dependence on an agency controlled by a person subject to the investigation.

### (b) FEDERAL ASSISTANCE.

Upon written request, a federal department or agency shall provide personnel, records, facilities, technical assistance, and other resources reasonably necessary to the investigation, subject to lawful privileges and judicial process.

### (c) DETAILED PERSONNEL.

Personnel detailed to the Special Prosecutor shall, for work within the assigned matter, be subject to the direction of the Special Prosecutor and may not be removed, reassigned, disciplined, denied access, or subjected to an adverse action because of lawful service to the investigation.

### (d) ACCESS DISPUTES.

A dispute concerning access to records, classified information, privileged material, personnel, or facilities shall be resolved on an expedited basis by a judge randomly assigned from the United States District Court for the District of Columbia who is not a member of the Special Division. Ordinary privileges, the Classified Information Procedures Act, grand-jury rules, and defendants’ rights remain applicable.

### (e) CLASSIFICATION.

Classification status alone shall not authorize an executive officer to deny the Special Prosecutor access to information within assigned jurisdiction. Access and use shall be subject to applicable security procedures and judicial protection.

## SEC. 11. INDEPENDENCE AND ANTI-EVASION.

### (a) PROHIBITED INTERFERENCE.

No officer or employee of the United States may knowingly—

1. obstruct or materially impede the Special Prosecutor;
2. conceal, destroy, alter, or withhold evidence or records within assigned jurisdiction;
3. direct an agency to deny lawful assistance;
4. retaliate against a person for providing lawful assistance or information;
5. use classification, reassignment, removal, funding, privilege, or administrative control for the purpose of frustrating the investigation; or
6. cause another person to engage in conduct prohibited by this subsection.

### (b) OTHER LAW UNAFFECTED.

Conduct prohibited by subsection (a) may be investigated and prosecuted under this Act and any otherwise applicable criminal, civil, administrative, or contempt authority.

### (c) NOTICE OF INTERFERENCE.

The Special Prosecutor shall promptly notify the Special Division and the appropriate committees of Congress of a substantial attempt to obstruct or disable the office, except that notice may be delayed when necessary to protect an ongoing investigation, grand-jury secrecy, national security, or individual rights.

## SEC. 12. REMOVAL AND DISCIPLINE.

### (a) EXCLUSIVE REMOVAL PROCEDURE.

The President, Attorney General, and other executive officers shall have no authority to remove, suspend, demote, reassign, or materially restrict the Special Prosecutor.

### (b) PETITION FOR REMOVAL.

A petition for removal may be filed under seal by—

1. the Attorney General;
2. the Inspector General of the Department of Justice;
3. the Chief Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit;
4. the Committee on the Judiciary of either House by majority vote; or
5. the Special Division on its own motion based on substantial and credible information.

### (c) CAUSE.

The Special Prosecutor may be removed only for—

1. permanent or substantial incapacity;
2. serious misconduct;
3. knowing and material violation of assigned jurisdiction;
4. substantial dereliction of duty;
5. conviction of a felony or offense involving dishonesty;
6. knowing and material abuse of the authority to depart from Department rules or policies; or
7. another condition rendering continued service incompatible with the integrity and independence of the office.

Disagreement with an investigative, charging, declination, litigation, reporting, or good-faith Department-policy departure decision does not constitute cause.

### (d) PROCEDURE.

The Special Division shall provide notice, an opportunity to respond, and, when material facts are disputed, an evidentiary hearing. A removal order shall contain written findings and shall be public to the maximum extent consistent with law.

### (e) EXPEDITED REVIEW.

The Special Prosecutor may seek direct expedited review of a removal order in the Supreme Court of the United States. A timely application filed within seven days shall stay removal for not more than fourteen days unless the Supreme Court orders otherwise.

### (f) TEMPORARY INCAPACITY.

During temporary incapacity or a stayed removal proceeding, the Special Division may designate an acting Special Prosecutor from among senior members of the office.

## SEC. 13. FUNDING.

### (a) APPROPRIATION.

There is appropriated from the Treasury, out of money not otherwise appropriated, such sums as are reasonably necessary to carry out this Act, subject to the limitations of this section.

### (b) ANNUAL LIMIT.

Expenditures for an appointment may not exceed $50,000,000 in a fiscal year, adjusted annually for inflation, unless the Special Division approves additional expenditures upon written findings of necessity and transmits those findings to the congressional appropriations and judiciary committees.

### (c) NO EXECUTIVE WITHHOLDING.

Funds lawfully available under this section may not be impounded, delayed, conditioned, transferred, or withheld by an executive officer.

### (d) AUDIT.

The Government Accountability Office shall audit expenditures at least annually without intruding into investigative or prosecutorial merits.

## SEC. 14. REPORTING AND IMPEACHMENT INFORMATION.

### (a) PERIODIC ADMINISTRATIVE REPORTS.

Every six months, the Special Prosecutor shall submit to the Special Division and the congressional judiciary committees a report concerning expenditures, staffing, general progress, jurisdictional status, and anticipated duration. The report shall not disclose information that would materially prejudice the investigation, violate grand-jury secrecy, compromise national security, or unfairly injure an uncharged person.

### (b) INFORMATION RELEVANT TO IMPEACHMENT.

If the Special Prosecutor obtains substantial and credible information indicating that the President may have committed conduct that could constitute grounds for impeachment, the Special Prosecutor shall transmit a confidential report and supporting materials to the Committee on the Judiciary of the House of Representatives.

### (c) NO AUTOMATIC IMPEACHMENT DETERMINATION.

A judicial determination that presidential or executive action was unlawful does not by itself establish an impeachable offense. The Special Prosecutor’s transmission under subsection (b) shall distinguish—

1. judicial findings of unlawfulness;
2. evidence of possible criminal conduct;
3. evidence bearing on intent, obstruction, abuse of power, corruption, or breach of public trust; and
4. unresolved factual and legal questions.

The House of Representatives retains exclusive constitutional authority to determine whether impeachment is warranted.

### (d) NO SUBSTITUTION FOR IMPEACHMENT.

1. Appointment, investigation, prosecution, reporting, or any other action under this Act does not remove, suspend, disqualify, or otherwise alter the tenure of the President or any other civil officer.
2. This Act does not authorize the Special Prosecutor, the Special Division, or any court to determine that an officer should be impeached, convicted in an impeachment trial, or removed from office.
3. This Act supplements the constitutional impeachment process by preserving and transmitting information that may be relevant to Congress; it does not bypass, replace, delay, or condition the exercise of impeachment authority.
4. Nothing in this Act requires the House to await completion of an investigation before exercising impeachment authority or requires a Special Prosecutor to await impeachment before conducting a lawful investigation.

### (e) GRAND-JURY MATERIAL.

Upon application by the Special Prosecutor, the district court supervising the relevant grand jury may authorize disclosure of grand-jury material to the House Committee on the Judiciary when necessary to perform the constitutional impeachment function, subject to protective conditions.

### (f) FINAL REPORT.

At the conclusion of the appointment, the Special Prosecutor shall prepare a report explaining the disposition of matters within jurisdiction. A public version shall be released after review for grand-jury secrecy, classified information, fair-trial rights, legitimate privacy interests, and protection of uncharged persons. Disputed redactions shall be resolved by a judge not serving on the Special Division.

## SEC. 15. STATUTES OF LIMITATION.

For an offense within assigned jurisdiction allegedly committed by a sitting President, any otherwise applicable limitation period shall be suspended during the President’s tenure in office and for 180 days thereafter, except to the extent prohibited by the Constitution.

## SEC. 16. COMPLETION AND TERMINATION.

### (a) COMPLETION BY SPECIAL PROSECUTOR.

The Special Prosecutor shall notify the Special Division when all matters within jurisdiction have been completed or transferred consistently with this Act.

### (b) TERMINATION BY SPECIAL DIVISION.

The Special Division may terminate an appointment only upon finding that—

1. the assigned matters have been completed;
2. no substantial investigative or prosecutorial work remains;
3. continuation is no longer necessary to address the extraordinary executive conflict; or
4. termination is required because no lawful jurisdiction remains.

### (c) NO MERITS SUBSTITUTION.

The Special Division may not terminate an appointment merely because it disagrees with the pace, theory, charging judgment, Department-policy departure, or litigation position of the Special Prosecutor.

### (d) PRESERVATION OF RECORDS.

Upon termination, all official records shall be transferred to the National Archives under secure procedures preserving grand-jury secrecy, classified information, law-enforcement sensitivity, congressional access authorized by law, and future judicial process.

## SEC. 17. JUDICIAL REVIEW.

### (a) EXPEDITED CONSIDERATION.

A court shall expedite any action concerning the constitutionality of this Act, the authority of the Special Prosecutor, access to evidence, removal, or enforcement of the appointment order.

### (b) NO INTERLOCUTORY DELAY BY TARGET.

Except as otherwise required by the Constitution, a person who is merely the subject or target of an investigation may not obtain interlocutory review of the appointment or jurisdiction before indictment or other judicially reviewable action.

### (c) DEFENDANT’S RIGHTS.

A criminal defendant may challenge the Special Prosecutor’s constitutional or statutory authority in the criminal proceeding to the same extent as any other jurisdictional or constitutional defense.

## SEC. 18. RULES OF CONSTRUCTION.

Nothing in this Act shall be construed to—

1. authorize the Special Division to exercise prosecutorial power;
2. permit Congress or a court to direct the disposition of a particular criminal case;
3. alter the constitutional authority of the House to impeach or the Senate to try impeachments;
4. establish that every unlawful executive action is criminal or impeachable;
5. make appointment or action under this Act a substitute for impeachment or removal under the Constitution;
6. prevent appointment of a special counsel or prosecutor from within the executive branch under otherwise lawful authority;
7. diminish the rights of an accused person;
8. authorize disclosure contrary to grand-jury secrecy, classified-information law, or a valid privilege except through lawful judicial process; or
9. alter substantive presidential immunity or the pardon power except as expressly provided by constitutional amendment.

## SEC. 19. SEVERABILITY.

If any provision of this Act or its application to any person or circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of this Act and its application to other persons and circumstances shall not be affected.

## SEC. 20. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect upon certification by the Archivist of the United States that the constitutional amendment authorizing judicial appointment of an independent special prosecutor has been ratified, and shall apply to conduct occurring before or after that date to the maximum extent permitted by the Constitution, except that no substantive criminal liability shall be imposed retroactively.

## Drafting Notes

- The judicial-referral provision is intentionally narrower than an automatic trigger. A court must identify both credible evidence of a possible federal offense and an extraordinary executive conflict.
- An adverse judicial ruling remains relevant evidence but is not treated as proof of criminality, corrupt intent, or impeachable conduct.
- The Special Division appoints and protects the prosecutor but does not manage the prosecution.
- The Act supplies multiple institutional access routes so a conflicted Attorney General cannot veto the mechanism.
- The Special Prosecutor must ordinarily follow Department rules and policies, but may depart for good cause through written notification rather than advance permission.
- Notice-only departure preserves prosecutorial independence while creating a record for transparency, final reporting, jurisdictional review, and removal for knowing and material abuse.
- The Act expressly authorizes investigation and evidence preservation concerning a sitting President while leaving indictment and trial during the presidential term to separate constitutional determination.
- The impeachment-report provision assists the House without transferring impeachment judgment to the prosecutor or judiciary.
- The judicial appointment mechanism is an alternative source of appointment in extraordinary conflict cases and does not eliminate ordinary executive-branch appointment authority.
- The Act does not independently resolve the scope of presidential criminal immunity or restrict the pardon power; those questions require separate constitutional treatment if the project chooses to address them.
