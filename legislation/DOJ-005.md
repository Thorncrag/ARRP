# DOJ-005 — Senior DOJ Recusal Review and Transparency Act

## A BILL

To improve documentation, independent review, transparency, and compliance for Department of Justice recusal decisions involving senior officials.

## SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the **“Senior DOJ Recusal Review and Transparency Act.”**

## SEC. 2. COVERED OFFICIALS.

This Act applies to the Attorney General, Deputy Attorney General, Solicitor General, Associate Attorney General, Assistant Attorneys General, United States Attorneys, the Director and Deputy Director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, and acting officials exercising substantially equivalent authority.

## SEC. 3. WRITTEN DISCLOSURE.

A covered official shall disclose in writing any relationship or interest that may require disqualification under applicable conflict, ethics, impartiality, or professional-responsibility rules. The disclosure shall identify the matter, relevant facts, governing standard, proposed scope of participation, and any requested authorization.

## SEC. 4. SENIOR DOJ RECUSAL REVIEW PANEL.

A decision involving recusal, nonrecusal, partial recusal, authorization to participate, or termination of recusal shall be made by a panel consisting of qualified career representatives of DOJ’s ethics office, DOJ OIG, and the Office of Government Ethics.

The affected official may provide information but may not select, remove, supervise, or direct panel members regarding the determination.

## SEC. 5. TEMPORARY TRANSFER OF AUTHORITY.

When a substantial and nonfrivolous recusal question is presented, the covered official shall temporarily cease participation and authority shall pass to the lawful successor until the panel issues a decision. The panel shall act promptly and ordinarily within seven days.

## SEC. 6. WRITTEN DETERMINATION.

Each determination shall state the governing authority, material facts, scope, duration, successor official, access restrictions, permitted contacts, and procedure for reconsideration.

A public version shall be released to the maximum extent consistent with lawful secrecy, privilege, investigative integrity, fair-trial rights, and legitimate privacy. An unredacted version shall be preserved and, for nationally significant matters, transmitted confidentially to the House and Senate Judiciary Committees.

## SEC. 7. EFFECT OF RECUSAL.

A recused official may not directly or indirectly supervise the matter; influence staffing, funding, jurisdiction, access, or case strategy; communicate a preferred outcome; receive nonpublic briefings except as specifically authorized; or cause another person to exercise authority on the official’s behalf.

## SEC. 8. AUTHORIZATION TO PARTICIPATE.

Any authorization shall be narrowly tailored, written, supported by specific findings, and limited in duration. Generalized claims of convenience, policy control, or personal confidence are insufficient.

## SEC. 9. MODIFICATION OR TERMINATION.

A recusal may be modified or terminated only through a new written panel determination based on materially changed facts or law. The affected official may not resume participation unilaterally.

## SEC. 10. COMPLIANCE AND CORRECTIVE ACTION.

Material nondisclosure, misrepresentation, participation contrary to a binding determination, retaliation against reviewing officials, or indirect evasion shall be referred to OIG and OPR. Corrective action may include removal from the matter, reassignment of authority, reconsideration of affected decisions, and appropriate personnel action under existing law.

The panel may not direct investigative or prosecutorial merits. Its authority is limited to deciding who may participate.

## SEC. 11. REPORTING.

DOJ shall publish annual aggregate information concerning covered determinations, authorizations, violations, corrective actions, and average resolution times. OIG shall report material interference with panel operations to the Judiciary Committees.

## SEC. 12. RELATION TO EXISTING LAW.

This Act supplements and does not narrow existing conflict-of-interest, recusal, ethics, professional-responsibility, whistleblower, civil-service, or Inspector General authorities.

## SEC. 13. SEVERABILITY.

If any provision or application is held invalid, the remainder shall not be affected.

## Budgetary Impact Statement

Preliminary ARRP assessment only; not a CBO, OMB, agency, or legislative-counsel score. Administrative workload is possible; no new appropriation is specified.

## Drafting Notes

- The proposal strengthens administration of existing duties rather than replacing them.
- Independent review is limited to participation and succession, not case merits.
- Temporary transfer prevents disputed participation while review is pending.
- Anti-evasion language addresses indirect supervision after formal recusal.
