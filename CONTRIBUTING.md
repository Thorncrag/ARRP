# Contributing

The American Restoration and Resilience Project is currently an author-directed public-policy research and drafting project. Public contributions may be invited to improve accuracy, sourcing, clarity, structure, and the quality of proposed institutional repairs.

## Current Contribution Status

The project is not yet formally released for broad public reuse. Until a public reuse license is adopted, contributions should be treated as proposed improvements to this repository, not as permission to reproduce, redistribute, or adapt the project elsewhere.

The project owner may accept, revise, decline, defer, or substantially rewrite any suggested contribution.

## Helpful Contributions

Helpful contributions include:

1. missing or better primary sources;
2. correction of factual, legal, citation, or formatting errors;
3. identification of duplicative, underdeveloped, or poorly scoped issues;
4. examples of institutional failures with reliable citations;
5. constitutional, statutory, administrative, or implementation constraints;
6. clearer drafting alternatives for issue analysis or proposed legislation; and
7. accessibility, readability, or navigation improvements.

## Source Expectations

Contributions that refer to real-world events, public actors, legal materials, government reports, court filings, legislation, journalism, institutional claims, or factual examples should include citations and links where available.

Prefer primary sources when they are available and practical, including statutes, regulations, official reports, court opinions, filings, congressional materials, agency documents, and original policy texts. Reliable secondary sources may be useful for context, especially when primary materials are unavailable or difficult to interpret.

## Authorship and Rights

By submitting a contribution, you represent that you have the right to submit it and that it does not knowingly include material you lack permission to contribute.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, submitted contributions may be incorporated, edited, rearranged, summarized, or declined by the project owner. Accepted contributions do not transfer authorship of the project as a whole.

Contributors retain any rights they have in their own original contributions, subject to the project owner's right to use, edit, and maintain accepted contributions in this repository and in later versions of the project. When the project later adopts a Creative Commons or other public reuse license, accepted contributions may be included in materials released under that license unless otherwise agreed before acceptance.

## Tone and Scope

Contributions should focus on institutional vulnerability, evidence, and repair. The project is not neutral about abuse or rule-of-law failure, but it applies standards prospectively and evenhandedly across parties, officeholders, and political circumstances.

Avoid unsupported accusations, personal attacks, partisan slogans, and proposed remedies tailored to punish or protect a named person rather than repair a structural defect.
