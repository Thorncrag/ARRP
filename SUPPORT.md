# Support ARRP

The American Restoration and Resilience Project is an independent, author-directed public-policy research and drafting project. Its public work is freely available to read, and its technical record remains open for inspection.

If you find the work useful and would like to help sustain its continued development, you may provide voluntary financial support. Support is appreciated, but it is never required to read the project, submit information, suggest a correction, or offer substantive feedback.

## What Support Helps Sustain

Voluntary support can help cover the ordinary costs of maintaining and developing the project, including:

- primary-source access and research tools;
- website, publication, and document-production infrastructure;
- source verification and project-integrity checks; and
- the time required to investigate, draft, revise, and maintain proposals.

## Funding and Editorial Independence

Financial support does not purchase editorial influence, favorable treatment, priority consideration, endorsement, access to unpublished conclusions, or authority over the project's research agenda. Supporters do not become members, clients, or owners of ARRP and receive no exclusive rights in project material.

The project will continue to identify its author, methods, material limitations, and relevant conflicts transparently. If its funding model later changes in a way that could reasonably affect a reader's assessment of the work, that change should be disclosed here.

## Public Access and Reuse

Support does not change the availability of ARRP's public materials or the rights and reuse terms stated in [Rights and Reuse](LICENSE.md). It does not narrow any public license applicable to project material or purchase ownership of the project's copyright.

ARRP plans to release appropriate original project material under a Creative Commons or comparable public reuse license once the project reaches a sufficiently mature and stable release stage. Until that license is formally adopted, the current terms in [Rights and Reuse](LICENSE.md) remain controlling.

## Tax Treatment

ARRP is not presently represented as a tax-exempt charitable organization. Financial support should not be treated as a tax-deductible charitable contribution.

Questions about support or the project's independence may be sent through the private [contact-the-author form](https://arrp-public-intake.vercel.app/?mode=contact&subject=ARRP%20support).
