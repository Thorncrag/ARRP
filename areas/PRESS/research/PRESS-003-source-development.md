# PRESS-003 Source Development

This record is source-development work, not a completed finding or a substitute for the proposal's verified manifestations, analysis, or citations. It preserves sources routed to this proposal for later source-specific review and integration.

## Air Force One Reporting Subpoenas

On July 10, 2026, federal agents reportedly served grand-jury subpoenas at the homes of four *New York Times* journalists after their reporting on security concerns involving the Qatari-provided aircraft placed into service as Air Force One. The subpoenas reportedly directed the journalists to testify before a federal grand jury and identified only an unspecified alleged violation of federal criminal law. The report also described a White House meeting involving Justice Department officials before the subpoenas issued. These allegations raise a compulsory-process concern but do not yet establish the suspected offense, the purpose of any White House communication, political direction, retaliatory motive, or noncompliance with DOJ rules. See [SRC-2630](https://apnews.com/article/e2c798a95a1e41077d2cae969774df91).

Current DOJ policy treats compulsory process against nonconsenting members of the news media as an extraordinary measure and prescribes Attorney General authorization, necessity, alternative-source, and tailoring safeguards. An April 2025 Attorney General memorandum reopened compulsory process to identify sources of improper leaks while retaining enhanced safeguards. Source development should obtain the subpoenas or motion-to-quash record, the alleged statutory violation, any Attorney General authorization, the alternatives-exhausted showing, the scope of requested testimony, and records of White House–DOJ contacts. See [SRC-2631](https://www.justice.gov/jm/jm-9-13000-obtaining-evidence#9-13.400) and [SRC-2632](https://www.justice.gov/oip/media/1399681/dl?inline=).

## Washington Post Reporter Search

The January 2026 FBI search of Washington Post reporter Hannah Natanson's home occurred in a classified-materials investigation involving a government contractor; reporting stated that Natanson and the Post were not targets and that agents seized journalistic devices and materials. Treat political motive, intimidation, or retaliation as concerns raised by critics rather than established findings unless court records, inspector-general materials, congressional findings, or official admissions substantiate them. See [SRC-0322](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jan/14/fbi-raid-washington-post-hannah-natanson), [SRC-0323](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/feb/24/judge-blocks-washington-post-reporter-search-devices-doj), and [SRC-0324](https://apnews.com/article/c4fcb4d718d8313940f7c19a8f3f8e26).

## Verification Needs

- Obtain controlling subpoenas, warrants, court orders, and docket materials rather than relying solely on reporting.
- Determine whether current approval, notice, exhaustion, and narrow-tailoring rules were followed and whether those safeguards are legally enforceable.
- Distinguish legitimate investigation of unlawful disclosure from compulsory process that unnecessarily burdens newsgathering or source confidentiality.
