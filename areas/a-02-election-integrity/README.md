# A-02 — Election Integrity

## Generalized Institutional Concern

The election system remains vulnerable to federal executive interference, delegitimization of lawful results, pressure on election officials, disruption of certification, structural distortions in presidential selection, and efforts to obstruct the peaceful transfer of power.

## Issues

- [ELEC-001 — Federal Executive Interference with State and Local Election Administration](issues/ELEC-001.md)
- **ELEC-002** — Pressure on certification and canvassing officials _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-003** — Insufficient protection for election workers and officials _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-004** — Abuse of post-election litigation and investigatory mechanisms _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-005** — Weak safeguards against alternate-elector or certification schemes _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-006** — Insufficiently automatic presidential-transition procedures _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-007** — Military or armed federal-force intervention in election administration _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **ELEC-008** — Weak accountability for efforts to overturn lawful results _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- [ELEC-009 — Electoral College Structure and National Popular Vote](issues/ELEC-009.md) _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_

## Issue Boundaries

- **ELEC-001** concerns civilian executive interference through presidential authority, agencies, investigations, funding, regulation, intelligence, administrative process, or official influence.
- **ELEC-002** concerns pressure upon officials exercising canvassing or certification duties, including pressure from actors outside the federal executive branch.
- **ELEC-005** concerns unlawful manipulation of the existing presidential-elector and certification process.
- **ELEC-007** is reserved for military, National Guard, or armed federal-force intervention in polling, counting, canvassing, recount, certification, or custody of election materials.
- **ELEC-009** concerns whether the Electoral College itself should be restructured or replaced by a national popular-vote system.

## Notes

ELEC-001 is developed. ELEC-009 has preliminary candidate framing. Remaining issues are provisional and require full source development and overlap review.
