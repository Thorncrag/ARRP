# A-17 — Independent Agencies and Regulatory Neutrality

## Generalized Institutional Concern

The constitutional and statutory position of independent agencies is increasingly unstable, while presidential control over removal, enforcement, adjudication, funding, and rulemaking may compromise functions intended to operate with some insulation from immediate partisan command.

- [**REG-001** — Erosion of Removal Protections for Independent Agencies](issues/REG-001.md) _(status: Candidate; priority: High)_
- **REG-002** — Politicized enforcement or nonenforcement _(status: Candidate; priority: Medium)_
- **REG-003** — Control of administrative adjudication _(status: Candidate; priority: Medium)_

- **REG-005** — Manipulation of commissions through vacancies _(status: Candidate; priority: Medium)_

## Issue Boundaries

- [REG-001](issues/REG-001.md) is the primary home for *Trump v. Slaughter* and the erosion of statutory for-cause removal protections for independent multimember agencies. The opinion should be read alongside [*Trump v. Cook*](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/25pdf/25a312_5468.pdf), which preserved a narrower Federal Reserve-specific interim protection lane grounded in central-bank history and statutory pretermination process. The July 2026 Election Assistance Commission removal-and-vacancy episode is a REG-001 source-development manifestation, with cross-references to ELEC-001 and ELEC-013 for election-administration and HAVA/candidate-access implications.

- **REG-004** is the primary home for [HOR-018](https://github.com/Thorncrag/ARRP/issues/16), the agency-continuity and executive anti-nullification horizon item. USAID and Department of Education weakening should be source-developed together where the shared defect is functional nullification of congressionally created agencies through reductions in force, transfers, funding freezes, program termination, closure of operational capacity, or refusal to preserve statutory-program capacity. Education-specific civil-rights or federalism questions may cross-reference A-24, A-11, and A-20, but the agency-continuity weakness belongs here unless later source development shows a distinct remedial architecture.

- **REG-004** and **REG-007** are the primary homes for [HOR-024](https://github.com/Thorncrag/ARRP/issues/235), the CFPB operational-dismantling and funding-leverage horizon item. The CFPB episode should be treated as a source-development manifestation of agency suspension or dismantling, independent-funding leverage, enforcement nullification, personnel hollowing, and public-information risk, not as a CFPB-only proposal unless later review shows a distinct statutory defect. Source leads include Associated Press reporting on the 2025 stop-work and building-closure directive and preliminary injunction, *Seila Law LLC v. CFPB*, *CFPB v. Community Financial Services Association*, Dodd-Frank's CFPB mission and funding provisions, and later reporting on public-information removals.
