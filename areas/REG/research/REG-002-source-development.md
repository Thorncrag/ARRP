# REG-002 Source Development

This internal record supports [REG-002](../issues/REG-002.md). It preserves routed material for later verification and does not convert allegations, pleadings, staff disagreement, suspicious timing, or political criticism into a finding of improper influence.

## Scope-Control Rule

An institution or episode belongs in REG-002 only when it concerns an identifiable noncriminal investigation, merger review, settlement, or enforcement disposition; the asserted weakness involves case-specific executive intervention or favored access outside an adequately reviewable process; and substantially the same transparency, preservation, conflict, oversight, or anti-retaliation safeguards could address it.

Do not route an episode here merely because an executive agency enforced or declined to enforce a law. General presidential directives, categorical program shutdowns, rulemaking, litigation positions, administrative adjudication, criminal prosecution, and retaliatory use of regulatory power require their own primary homes.

## Legacy Routing Re-Adjudication

Earlier automated source routing associated broad Clean Air Act policy, wind-energy approval actions, and general litigation trackers with REG-002. Those materials do not support the narrowed proposal merely because they involve agency action. The clear general-policy and categorical-program records have been rerouted to REG-001 and REG-006; the [DEA enforcement docket (SRC-0959)](https://www.courtlistener.com/docket/73117487/richie-enterprises-llc-v-bondi/) remains provisional pending a case-specific influence review; and genuinely unresolved immigration-enforcement material remains in the pending-source process. None appears as a REG-002 manifestation without source-specific verification.

## Open Research Questions

1. What White House and agency contact policies currently govern DOJ Antitrust, FTC, FCC, EPA, and comparable civil-enforcement matters?
2. Which contacts, staff recommendations, declination memoranda, settlement communications, and conflict records are already preserved, disclosed, privileged, or protected by statute or regulation?
3. Where do inspector-general jurisdiction, attorney-client privilege, deliberative-process protection, grand-jury secrecy, commercial confidentiality, and ongoing-investigation interests limit review or disclosure?
4. Can Congress create a uniform internal record and protected-review obligation while leaving substantive enforcement discretion and agency-specific standards intact?
5. Which cross-administration examples demonstrate a recurring structural problem rather than an administration-specific controversy?
