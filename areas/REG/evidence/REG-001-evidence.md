# REG-001 Evidence Record

This page supplements [REG-001 — Agency Independence and Functional Nullification](../issues/REG-001.md). The issue page remains authoritative for the diagnosis, two-tier framework, remedies, legislation, score, and conclusions.

## Record Scope

This record preserves verified episodes, judicial dispositions, counterevidence, and monitoring predicates concerning presidential control or functional disablement of congressionally structured agencies. A threshold dismissal is not treated as approval of the challenged presidential authority.

## Judicial and Official Dispositions

### Executive Order 14215 and the Federal Election Commission

The Democratic National Committee challenged Executive Order 14215 before the order had been applied to alter a Federal Election Commission interpretation or enforcement decision. The District Court for the District of Columbia [dismissed the challenge](https://www.fec.gov/documents/5867/usdcdc-mem-opinion-06-03-2025.pdf) for lack of standing and ripeness after executive-branch and FEC counsel represented that the order had not been, and was not planned to be, used to control the Commission's interpretation of the Federal Election Campaign Act.

## Evidentiary Significance

The decision does not approve presidential control of the FEC's legal judgment. It is counterevidence against describing the executive order alone as a completed functional nullification of the Commission. The court treated changed circumstances or a concrete application as potentially supporting a later challenge, making the episode a defined monitoring item for REG-001 and an adjacent campaign-finance institutional record for ELEC-012.

## Monitoring Items

- Any directive, OMB or White House review, enforcement intervention, removal, vacancy practice, quorum loss, budget restriction, or formal legal-position change applying Executive Order 14215 to the FEC.
- Any later judicial decision addressing a concrete application rather than the pre-enforcement posture reviewed here.
