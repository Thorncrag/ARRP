### 2026-07-13 — T2 development audit

**Audit type:** Full T2 development audit under rubric `2026-06-27.2`.

**Score:** 75/100, Review Ready.

**Component score:** Structural 8/8; Evidence 8/12; Legal Fit 9/10; Prior-Proposal 6/8; Remedy 10/12; Implementation 6/8; Abuse Resistance 7/8; Drafting 7/8; Cogency 6/6; Adoption 4/12; Project Integration 4/4; External Review 0/4; Penalties 0.

**Current-status and manifestation finding:** T2 retained the post-*Slaughter* and *Cook* framing and verified a representative Tier One organic statute. 52 U.S.C. § 20923 confirms that Congress gave the EAC four Senate-confirmed members, political-balance and congressional-recommendation rules, fixed terms, and holdover service. Those features establish structural-independence and continuity indicators but do not themselves decide post-*Slaughter* removal power or guarantee quorum. GAO's June 2026 workforce report supplies current cross-government scale evidence while remaining explicitly insufficient to establish disablement at any particular agency. The catalog remains mixed: several entries have primary judicial or executive sources, while others still rely on secondary reporting or unresolved litigation.

**Cause, jurisdiction, and waiver correction:** Section 9 now creates an express cause arising under REG-001, gives D.D.C. exclusive original jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1331, and waives federal sovereign immunity only for relief other than money damages. Mandatory decrees must identify responsible officers. Retrospective damages and claims arising solely under another law remain outside the route, preventing accidental Tucker Act, APA, civil-service, or organic-statute displacement.

**Standing and congressional-plaintiff correction:** T2 removed the either-House and committee route. Congress may sue only through an officer designated after each House authorizes the same action and only if Congress independently satisfies Article III. States, regulated or benefited persons, grantees, contractors, covered entities, members, officers, and the Comptroller General still require concrete injury and otherwise valid capacity or statutory authority. *Raines* risk therefore remains a real limit rather than being treated as legislatively curable.

**Three-judge and appeal correction:** The clerk now randomly assigns the initial D.D.C. judge; that judge, not the clerk, notifies the D.C. Circuit chief judge unless the required claim is wholly insubstantial or frivolous under *Shapiro* and 28 U.S.C. § 2284. Before panel formation, relief is a Rule 65(b) temporary restraining order, and the single judge may not adjudicate ordinary dismissal, preliminary injunction, permanent injunction, or merits issues. Section 1253 is expressly displaced; final appeals proceed under section 1291, interlocutory injunction appeals under section 1292, and Supreme Court certiorari under section 1254.

**Scope and anti-abuse correction:** T2 defined a major covered measure through duration, quorum or adjudication, nationwide or material-class effects, a $100 million fiscal threshold, and imminent irreversible capacity loss, with aggregation to prevent segmentation. The preservation duty now begins upon service of a complaint or a particularized GAO or inspector-general certification rather than vague notice of a credible claim. It is limited to reasonably identifiable material within possession, custody, or control and preserves law, privilege, and unrelated records schedules. Specialized exhaustion, channeling, limitations, and exclusive-review rules remain applicable to claims arising solely under other law; relabeling cannot evade them, but a genuinely independent REG-001 claim remains available.

**Article II and remedy finding:** The bill continues to preserve lawful presidential appointment, removal, supervision, reprioritization, reorganization, enforcement discretion, foreign-affairs, military, and other constitutional authority. Relief cannot recreate invalid removal protection, compel a nomination, impose a historical staffing floor, dictate a discretionary outcome, award damages, or assume management. Relief ordinarily runs against subordinate responsible officers, and the bill does not authorize coercive relief against the President. GAO and inspector-general certifications remain nonbinding, avoiding executive execution by a congressional officer.

**Prior-proposal and implementation finding:** H.R. 1196 supplies a current agency-preservation analogue for USAID; H.R. 2456 supplies a contrary legislated-liquidation model for Education; historical reorganization-authority proposals show a congressionally approved restructuring path; GAO-18-427 supplies implementation-planning criteria; and B-332704 supplies a direct prior-consultation analogue and legislative-veto warning. Together they support enacted authorization, impact assessment, certification, continuity planning, and monitoring without validating REG-001's precise cross-government cause or three-judge route.

**Budget finding:** The proposal retains authorizations of such sums, no direct budget authority, and no new judgeships. Workload categories are now explicit: universal inventories and plans; legal, records, systems, and program staff; GAO and inspector-general review; clerk and panel work; protected records; discovery or special-master support; and compliance monitoring. The Judiciary's $17.2 million and 170-FTE workload-change item, GAO's 2,600-program inventory, and GAO's government-reorganization work are scale comparators only. No source supports a REG-001 dollar estimate, caseload, agency-hour estimate, or staffing number.

**Enactment-pathway finding:** Adoption increases from 3 to 4 because current Congresses have introduced both preservation and abolition vehicles, proving that the underlying institutional choice is legislatively cognizable. Neither current analogue advanced beyond introduction at T2 review, and no proposal-specific bipartisan coalition or external endorsement exists. Friction remains 88/100, Extreme Resistance; the required environment remains `sixty-vote-senate`; viability remains `plausible-after-wave`; and staged inventories, notice, preservation, and agency-specific continuity remain the most plausible narrower entry point.

**Score effect:** REG-001 advances from 63 to 75 and reaches Review Ready. The 12-point gain reflects primary manifestation and analogue development, correction of jurisdiction, waiver, standing, section 2284, Rule 65, appeal, specialized-review, preservation, scope, and presidential-relief defects, plus an explicit workload analysis. The remaining ceiling reflects incomplete primary verification across the catalog, unresolved application-specific Article II questions, no proposal-specific fiscal model, weak adoption evidence, and no external review.

**Next audit:** Full T3 readiness audit. Refresh all current case and legislative postures; conduct a claim-by-claim source and legal durability table; test each deadline and emergency extension; inspect overlap, severability, effective-date, protected-information, class, intervention, consolidation, stay, and enforcement mechanics; verify legislative form and cross-references; resolve any remaining publication-blocking ambiguity; and recalculate without credit for unresolved or secondary-only claims.

**Framework finding:** The issue performs every required analytical function: concise snapshot, institutional anomaly, two-tier scope, shared threshold and exclusions, sourced manifestations, damage, weakness, proposal survey, least-complex remedy, repair and prevention, clearly separated preferred and independent legislation paths, adjacent-proposal boundaries, two budget pathways, scoring, annotation, source notes, and a linked audit sidecar. The independent bill supplies definitions, substantive standards, continuity planning, notice, certification, preservation, cause of action, plaintiffs, three-judge procedure, relief, appeal, reporting, appropriations, construction, severability, and effective date.

**Current-status finding:** The issue is not moot. *Trump v. Slaughter* materially narrows conventional for-cause removal protection for the FTC and similarly situated executive officers; *Trump v. Cook* preserves a distinct Federal Reserve lane; and the catalog contains current Tier One and Tier Two removal, vacancy, quorum, reduction-in-force, stop-work, transfer, and dismantling episodes. The controlling doctrinal change requires function-based continuity safeguards rather than a return to a universal independent-agency removal model.

**Source finding:** T1 confirmed primary current sources for *Slaughter*, *Cook*, the Take Care Clause, APA chapter 7, sections 47, 1657, and 2284 of title 28, and several judicial and executive Tier Two records. Much of the manifestation catalog remains secondary or only partially reviewed, and several district or appellate matters remain preliminary. T2 must replace or supplement catalog reporting with organic statutes, removal notices, dockets, final orders, agency records, GAO or inspector-general material, and current operational evidence before broad factual claims receive full credit.

**Preliminary legal-fit finding:** The strongest statutory lane is Congress's authority to create institutions, assign mandatory duties, regulate appropriations and administration, and supply judicial causes and remedies, paired with the Take Care duty. The bill appropriately preserves lawful removal, appointment, supervision, reorganization, enforcement discretion, and policy choice; requires substantial and sustained inability rather than staffing change alone; excludes direct control of discretionary outcomes; and limits final relief to lawful capacity. T2 must test sovereign-immunity waiver, subject-matter jurisdiction, Article III standing, institutional and congressional plaintiffs, Comptroller General authority, section 2284 single-judge limits, section 1253 displacement, section 47 disqualification, subordinate-officer relief, APA and specialized-review interaction, and Article II applications after *Slaughter*.

**Remedy-fit finding:** JUD-011 alone remains the preferred cross-subject anti-nullification remedy. REG-001 alone is a complete independent alternative if Congress rejects JUD-011. The two-tier continuity rule fits the issue better than a blanket staffing floor or universal removal restriction: Tier One preserves lawful structural continuity without recreating unconstitutional tenure protection; Tier Two reaches only practical suspension of mandatory functions. Mechanism-specific appropriations, personnel, enforcement, records, and agency-funding issues remain in their adjacent proposal homes.

**Drafting and budget correction:** Corrected the purposes list and removed unsupported direct appropriations of $2 million startup and $5 million annually. Section 13 now authorizes such sums for fiscal years 2027 through 2033, states that it provides no budget authority, preserves AOUSC administration and anti-impoundment treatment for any later appropriation, and reserves fixed amounts for source-backed workload development. T2 must develop Judiciary, GAO, inspector-general, agency, secure-record, special-master, and continuity-planning workload analogues.

**Enactment-pathway finding:** An ordinary federal statute matches the legal vehicle, but enactment likely requires a sixty-vote Senate environment and unusually strong unified support. The proposal cuts across presidents' institutional interests and spans Judiciary, Oversight, appropriations, authorizing, personnel, and agency-specific jurisdictions. Adoption is 3/12; friction is 88/100, Extreme Resistance; pathway viability is `plausible-after-wave`; development priority is `active`; and pathway adjustment is `stage`. A narrower initial package could focus on inventories, advance notice, preservation, reporting, and agency-specific quorum or succession safeguards.

**Score effect:** The first scored audit replaces the candidate fixed zero with 63/100, Developed Draft. The score recognizes complete architecture, a coherent two-tier remedy, current controlling doctrine, strong integration, and a functioning standalone bill. It remains below Review Ready because manifestation verification, legal durability, direct prior proposals, implementation evidence, cost evidence, adoption support, and external review are incomplete.

**Next audit:** Full T2 development audit. Verify organic statutes and current case posture across representative Tier One and Tier Two institutions; resolve cause, jurisdiction, waiver, standing, panel, appeal, relief, Comptroller General, APA, specialized-review, and Article II questions; identify direct and functional prior proposals; test inventory, notice, certification, emergency, reporting, and continuity deadlines; develop source-backed budget and workload analogues; assess adoption and opposition evidence; revise the bill; and recalculate under the current rubric.

### 2026-07-13 — JUD-011 cross-issue consistency Change Audit

**Audit type:** Targeted Change Audit and Internal Remedy-Fit review; no scored quality audit.

**Scope:** Compared REG-001's anomaly, two-tier manifestations, damage, weakness, proposal survey, preferred remedy, repair and prevention, pathway presentation, budgets, annotation, audit metadata, and area summary against JUD-011 and the standalone Congressional Institutional Continuity and Anti-Nullification Act.

**Coverage finding:** REG-001 remains a proper automatic civil application. Its covered cases require an enacted institutional or mandatory-function command, executive action or deliberate inaction, substantial and sustained practical disablement rather than ordinary supervision, preserved lawful discretion, a concretely injured plaintiff, and relief capable of restoring statutory capacity. The issue page does not suggest that every removal, vacancy, staffing decision, or policy disagreement satisfies JUD-011.

**Independent-remedy finding:** The standalone Act independently supplies its two-tier rule, notice and preservation duties, plaintiff categories, three-judge D.D.C. procedure, emergency authority, merits standard, relief, appeal, reporting, and appropriations. It contains no JUD-011 prerequisite or fallback. JUD-011 alone remains the preferred general remedy; REG-001 alone remains the independent alternative.

**Corrections and budget finding:** Replaced the ambiguous standalone-only front-matter vehicle field with explicit preferred and alternative proposal fields; updated the stale rubric label from `2026-06-27.1` to the current `2026-06-27.2`; standardized reader-facing references as “Interbranch Review Framework Act (JUD-011)” and the alternative-path formulation as “if Congress rejects”; and confirmed separate preferred-path and standalone-path budget statements. These are consistency corrections and do not constitute a scored T1 review.

### 2026-07-13 — JUD-011-primary and standalone-alternative architecture review

**Audit type:** Targeted Change Audit and Internal Remedy-Fit review; no scored quality audit.

**Scope:** Corrected REG-001's enactment architecture. JUD-011 alone is the preferred generally applicable remedy, while the [Congressional Institutional Continuity and Anti-Nullification Act](../../../legislation/REG-001.md) is a separate standalone alternative with its own complete three-judge D.D.C. procedure and no JUD-011 dependency.

**Internal Remedy-Fit finding:** The vehicle implements the same institutional defect described by the issue. Tier One preserves constitutionally permissible quorum, holdover, succession, records, and adjudicatory continuity without recreating invalid removal protection. Tier Two reaches only substantial and sustained inability to perform mandatory statutory functions. The bill aggregates coordinated measures, excludes ordinary management and discretionary enforcement choices, coordinates with appropriations and personnel law, and limits relief to restoration of lawful capacity.

**Interbranch-review finding:** A REG-001-type dispute falls within JUD-011 because substantial and sustained executive disablement can nullify an enacted institution or mandatory duty. JUD-011 itself supplies the preferred claim and remedy without REG-001 designation or enactment.

**Independent-remedy finding:** REG-001 now contains no JUD-011 routing, fallback, or transition clause. It independently supplies its two-tier continuity rule, cause of action, plaintiffs, standing limits, merits standard, defenses, relief, random D.D.C. assignment, section 2284 panel designation, emergency preservation, expedition, fact development, public and protected-record procedures, D.C. Circuit review, judicial reporting, and protected funding.

**Pathway-presentation finding:** The Proposed Legislation section now states the congressional choice directly: enact JUD-011 alone, or enact REG-001 alone. These are not two modes of one substantive bill and neither proposal is a prerequisite to the other.

**Budget finding:** The issue page separately states the two budget pathways. JUD-011 carries the shared judicial-system costs if Congress selects the general remedy, while case-specific agency-restoration and compliance costs remain outside its judicial appropriation. The standalone REG-001 draft uses provisional direct appropriations of $2 million for judicial readiness and $5 million annually for 7 fiscal years for its independent proceedings, without adding judgeships. Those planning placeholders require AOUSC, CBO, GAO, appropriations, PAYGO, and legislative-counsel review.

**Score effect:** No score assigned. REG-001 remains a fixed-zero candidate until a T1 audit performs the required current-status, source, legal-fit, prior-proposal, implementation, budget, adoption, and component-scoring checks. The prior `change_audit_needed` marker is resolved for proposal-to-draft alignment; T1 remains next.

### 2026-07-10 — Scope consolidation: two-tier independence and continuity framework

**Audit status:** Substantive issue revision recorded; targeted Change Audit and Internal Remedy-Fit Audit required.

**Scope:** Reorganized REG-001 around one shared defect: presidential action or deliberate inaction that functionally nullifies a congressionally established institution or mandatory executive function. Added a higher-review Tier One for entities Congress intended to operate with meaningful independence and a lower-review Tier Two for ordinary executive-controlled agencies and functions.

**Boundary finding:** Consolidated provisional REG-004 operational-dismantling and REG-005 vacancy-manipulation candidates into REG-001. HOR-018 now supplies the Tier Two Department of Education and USAID source-development lane; HOR-024 supplies the CFPB bridge between removal, funding, personnel, enforcement, and operational disablement. Mechanism-specific remedies remain cross-referenced to FUND, CIV, REG-002, REG-006, REG-007, ELEC, and FRB rather than duplicated.

**Remedy-development note:** The working remedy is now a two-tier statutory framework. Tier One would apply stronger continuity, notice, quorum, succession, records, and review safeguards where Congress demonstrated independence; Tier Two would trigger only upon substantial and sustained inability to perform mandatory statutory duties. Ordinary supervision, lawful priority setting, temporary vacancies, authorized reorganization, and discretionary functions remain outside the proposed threshold.

**Internal Remedy-Fit status:** Not run contemporaneously. The next pass must test legal fit after *Slaughter*, source sufficiency for the Tier Two manifestations, the constitutional and statutory basis for a continuity cause of action, standing and relief, overlap with existing mechanism-specific proposals, and whether cross-cutting or agency-specific legislation is least complex.

**Score effect:** No score change and no audit-count increment. The issue remains a candidate with a fixed zero score pending development; `change_audit_needed` is set to `true`.

### 2026-07-10 — Source-development manifestation: EAC removals and vacancy collapse

**Audit status:** Source-development manifestation added; no proposal-quality score assigned.

**Scope:** Added the July 2026 Election Assistance Commission removal-and-vacancy episode as a REG-001 manifestation after current reporting that President Trump removed Democratic EAC commissioners Thomas Hicks and Benjamin Hovland following *Trump v. Slaughter*, Republican commissioner Christy McCormick resigned, and Republican commissioner Donald Palmer had already stepped down, leaving the four-member EAC without commissioners.

**Boundary finding:** REG-001 is the primary home because the structural defect is removal-power exposure and independent-commission design after *Slaughter*. ELEC-001 and ELEC-013 receive cross-references for election-administration and HAVA/candidate-access implications, but they should not absorb the independent-agency removal problem unless later source development shows a distinct election-remedy lane.

**Catalog update:** Created [REG-001 Independent-Agency Removal and Vacancy Catalog](../../../source-development/REG-001-independent-agency-removal-catalog.md) as the running list for reported removals, attempted removals, resignation demands, resignations, and vacancy-collapse episodes involving independent or quasi-independent boards and commissions. Initial rows cover PCLOB, NLRB, MSPB, FLRA, FEC, EEOC, FTC, CPSC, FRB/Cook, and EAC source leads, with a protection/status column to identify why some attempts failed, were delayed, or remained agency-specific rather than generalizable.

**Remedy-development note:** Added vacancy non-refill as a distinct anti-nullification theory for source development. REG-001 should evaluate whether removal plus refusal to nominate or refill seats can disable an agency that Congress required to exist and whether statutory remedies should include quorum-preservation, holdover, nomination/reporting, acting-service, and expedited-review safeguards.

**Score effect:** No score change and no audit-count increment.
