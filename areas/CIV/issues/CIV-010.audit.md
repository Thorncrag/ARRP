# CIV-010 — Audit History

This file preserves the full review history for [CIV-010](CIV-010.md). The issue page keeps only the compact Proposal Scoring summary.

## Audit History

### 2026-07-23 — Horizon candidate admission and foundation decision

**Audit status:** Foundation approved; initial statutory development pending; no proposal-quality score assigned

**Proposal-quality score:** 0

**Runs:** No change. Candidate adjudication, admission, foundation approval, and source development are not T0–T4 issue-quality audits.

**Admission finding:** HOR-034 satisfies the issue-admission test as a distinct government-wide purpose-limitation problem. CIV-009 owns the authority and safeguards of the unit obtaining access; CIV-010 owns whether government-held personal data may be used for a materially different purpose that Congress did not explicitly authorize. DOM-009, ELEC-001, RIGHTS-001, and REC-001 retain their specialized surveillance, election, civil-rights, and records-integrity questions.

**Political-failure and transparency finding:** The project is taking an affirmative policy position rather than claiming that every challenged secondary use violates present law. The public issue page must disclose that choice up front. A violation corrected through timely and effective judicial relief does not alone establish an institutional failure; the proposal addresses the broader absence, inconsistency, or practical unenforceability of a congressional-authorization baseline.

**Manifestation finding:** Current tax, benefits, personnel, and cross-agency data disputes show that purpose authority and remedies depend on different program statutes, exceptions, and judicial routes. The D.C. Circuit's treatment of qualifying tax-address requests demonstrates that Congress may authorize a defined secondary use. The SNAP-data preliminary injunction illustrates a program-specific use limitation. Pending DOGE, personnel, and cross-agency matters remain allegations or developing records and are not treated as final findings of illegality.

**Human-governed foundation:** On July 23, 2026, the human author approved: (1) the fragmented government-wide purpose-limitation failure; (2) current cross-program data-sharing manifestations; (3) a prohibition on federal use of personal data for purposes Congress has not explicitly authorized, with necessity, minimization, independent review, retention limits, and prospective relief; and (4) a federal legislative vehicle consisting of a Privacy Act amendment plus necessary conforming amendments to program-specific personal-data statutes.

**Initial remedy finding:** The least-complex adequate architecture begins with one government-wide Privacy Act rule. It must prevent an executive order, interagency agreement, regulation, system-of-records notice, or agency-defined routine use from independently authorizing a materially different purpose. A conforming review is required because stronger restrictions and particular permissions are distributed across federal statutes.

**Unresolved finding:** Development must define covered personal data, original and materially related purposes, necessary program administration, derived data, same-department transfers, contractors, research, anti-fraud, emergency, law-enforcement, oversight, records-retention, archival, and legal-hold treatment. It must also test prospective remedies, sovereign immunity, standing, reviewability, classified and law-enforcement-sensitive information, existing stronger protections, administrative burden, and constitutional separation-of-powers objections.

**Closeout status:** Admission and the four-part foundation are complete. The proposal enters `Development level: In development` and `Status: Development`. It remains unscored with zero Runs because the initial bill and statute-by-statute analysis are not yet complete enough for a productive T-audit.

**Next review need:** Complete a statute-first Privacy Act and program-statute survey, define the purpose and retention rules, identify conforming amendments and exceptions, develop effective prospective relief, and draft the initial federal vehicle before assigning the first T-audit.
