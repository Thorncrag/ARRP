# CIV-010 — Congressional Authorization for Secondary Use of Government-Held Personal Data

> **Source-development record:** This record assembles source-specific research admitted from `HOR-034`. It is source-development work, not a completed project finding or a determination that the described conduct is unlawful.

Each entry states the proposition presently under review. `Reviewed: No` means that proposition still requires source-level verification or qualitative placement. A monitoring qualification identifies the future development being watched; it does not establish that the source presently supports a final conclusion.

## Human-Approved Policy Foundation

On July 23, 2026, the human author approved the following foundational policy principle and admitted the candidate as `CIV-010`:

> The federal government should not use personal data for purposes Congress has not explicitly authorized.

For implementation, using data to administer the congressionally authorized purpose for which it was collected does not create a new purpose merely because incidental administrative operations are required. A materially unrelated secondary use, however, requires specific statutory authorization. Independent review, necessity and minimization requirements, and an effective prospective remedy are safeguards governing an authorized secondary-use regime; they are not substitutes for congressional authorization.

This is an affirmative ARRP policy judgment. It is disclosed up front in the admitted public proposal and must not be presented as though existing law already establishes the principle in full. Particular incidents must still be characterized separately as alleged violations of existing law, uses outside a congressionally defined program scope, or uses permitted because existing law lacks the proposed safeguard.

## Provisional Cross-Cutting Remedy Architecture

The likely primary vehicle is an amendment to the Privacy Act establishing a clear government-wide prohibition: an agency may not use or disclose personal data for a materially different purpose unless an Act of Congress specifically authorizes that purpose. An executive order, interagency agreement, agency regulation, or agency-defined routine use should not independently create the necessary authorization.

Because federal data rules are distributed across program-specific statutes, the baseline amendment would likely require conforming review and, where necessary, amendments governing particular categories of personal data. Those amendments should:

1. identify the congressionally authorized collection and use purposes;
2. reconcile existing disclosure exceptions with the government-wide prohibition;
3. limit retention to periods justified by an authorized purpose, subject to expressly defined archival, evidentiary, legal-hold, and similar obligations;
4. provide necessity, minimization, notice, logging, and independent-review safeguards for authorized secondary uses; and
5. provide an effective prospective remedy when an agency uses or discloses data without the required authorization.

Congress may authorize particular secondary uses or defined categories of use. The proposed rule assigns that policy decision to Congress; it does not categorically prohibit Congress from making it. This remedy architecture is the approved development direction. Its precise statutory design remains provisional pending statute-by-statute review and legislative development.
