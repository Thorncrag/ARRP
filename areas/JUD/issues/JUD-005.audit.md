# JUD-005 Audit History

## Audit History

### 2026-07-14 — Initial Development and Issue-Admission Review

**Audit classification:** Initial development and issue-admission review; not a T0–T4 audit and does not increment Runs.

**Admission result:** The earlier formulation—weak or partisan congressional response to judicial defiance—risked treating congressional political will as the institutional defect. The admitted issue is narrower and nonpartisan: federal law lacks a general automatic route that sends a court's qualifying executive-noncompliance finding simultaneously to congressional majority and minority recipients and maintains an accurate public record of later appellate or compliance status.

**Current-law review:** The review identified 18 U.S.C. § 401 and Federal Rule of Criminal Procedure 42 as the general contempt authorities. Neither reviewed provision contains the proposed general congressional-notice and central-register duty. The review also identified 28 U.S.C. §§ 360 and 604 as direct administrative analogues for public availability of specified judicial orders and Administrative Office reporting, compilation, and congressional transmission duties.

**Remedy-fit result:** The proposal does not compel Congress to investigate or act. The preferred coordinated remedy uses JUD-001 for the qualifying finding, direct enforcement, transmission, and public register. DOJ-007 is only a conditional escalation path when its independent serious-misconduct and extraordinary-conflict requirements are satisfied. The standalone alternative remedies the information-routing defect without depending on any other ARRP proposal.

**Overlap result:** JUD-001 remains the home for judicial enforcement. DOJ-007 remains the home for extraordinary independent investigation. JUD-011 remains the home for executive nullification of congressional mandates. Congressional political response remains outside scope.

**Budget result:** Two separate preliminary pathways are stated. The preferred path adds incremental public-notice administration to JUD-001 and incurs DOJ-007 costs only upon separate activation. The independent bill requires limited clerk, Administrative Office, redaction, secure-transmission, and technology work but creates no new adjudicative or investigative institution. No dollar estimate is assigned before workload review.

**Score effect:** The issue remains at 0/100 under the fixed-zero candidate rule pending a completed T1 audit.

**Next audit need:** Run T1 on statutory placement, trigger precision, recipient definitions, public and protected versions, status updates, AOUSC implementation, JUD-001 and DOJ-007 proposal-to-legislation alignment, budget posture, navigation, and source inventory.
