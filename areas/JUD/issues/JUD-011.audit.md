### 2026-07-13 — JUD-011 cross-issue consistency Change Audit

**Audit type:** Project-wide Change Audit and targeted cross-issue Internal Remedy-Fit review; no scored quality audit.

**Scope:** Reviewed the governing anti-nullification framework, coverage matrix, JUD-011 issue and Act, REG-001 and FUND-001 automatic civil applications, DOJ-007 shared-infrastructure implementation, all four audit histories, affected area and legislation indexes, budget-path presentation, source inventory, and GitHub issue wrappers.

**Governing-rule finding:** The architecture is internally consistent. REG-001 and FUND-001 arise directly under JUD-011 when the statutory elements and Article III requirements are met; neither opts in. DOJ-007 does not arise under section 2303 and uses shared infrastructure only through its amendment and express implementing Act. The matrix exclusions remain consistent with the governing coverage test.

**Remedy-fit and vehicle finding:** JUD-011 supplies a complete general civil cause, procedure, relief, administration, and funding. REG-001 and FUND-001 each retain a complete standalone alternative. DOJ-007 retains one common amendment, a preferred JUD-011-dependent implementing Act, and a standalone Act that does not depend on JUD-011. No cross-path jurisdiction, decisional authority, protected records, or appellate route is transferred.

**Budget and integration finding:** REG-001, FUND-001, and DOJ-007 each contain two separately labeled budget statements. Shared, incremental, function-specific, and standalone costs are distinguished. Corrected affected-issue proposal metadata, REG-001's stale rubric label, audit-sidecar heading drift, and minor DOJ-007 presentation artifacts. Local links and retained source records remain aligned.

**Score effect and unresolved work:** No score or rebaseline change. JUD-011 remains fixed-zero pending T1; REG-001 remains fixed-zero pending T1; FUND-001 remains 60/100 pending T2; DOJ-007 remains 78/100 pending external validation and T4 follow-up. Legislative-counsel, AOUSC, CBO, standing, assignment, appellate, constitutional, and cost review remain at the next tiers identified on the issue pages.

### 2026-07-13 — Shared-infrastructure extension and DOJ-007 integration review

**Audit type:** Project-wide Change Audit plus unscored JUD-011 framework review.

**Scope:** Preserved JUD-011's generally applicable civil anti-nullification cause while making explicit its existing authority to host a specialized component added by a later Act and supported by independent constitutional authority. Added DOJ-007 as the first shared-infrastructure application after ratification of its amendment.

**Boundary finding:** DOJ-007 does not satisfy or arise under section 2303. Its amendment supplies the authority for nonexecutive appointment and protection of investigative counsel. The preferred implementing Act uses JUD-011 only for the national roster, clerk, facilities, security, technology, judgeships, financial administration, and other nondecisional capacity.

**Firewall finding:** Civil and investigative matters may not be consolidated. They use different panels, decisional employees, dockets, protected records, authority, and appellate routes. Participation in a related civil proceeding disqualifies a judge or decisional employee from investigative-counsel appointment, access, removal, and completion matters and from resulting criminal cases.

**Pathway finding:** REG-001 and FUND-001 retain the automatic civil-coverage pattern: JUD-011 alone is preferred and the subject bill alone is the alternative. DOJ-007 uses the express shared-infrastructure pattern: the amendment is common; JUD-011 plus the preferred implementing Act is the preferred path; the amendment plus standalone DOJ-007 Act is the independent path.

**Budget finding:** Every affected issue page now separates preferred-path and independent-alternative budget impacts. JUD-011's base appropriation does not automatically fund investigative operations; DOJ-007 must expressly authorize incremental shared costs and separately account for counsel and investigation-specific expenses.

**Score effect:** No score assigned. JUD-011 remains fixed-zero pending its T1 framework and quality audit.

### 2026-07-13 — No-opt-in architecture correction and Internal Remedy-Fit review

**Audit type:** Project-wide Change Audit plus unscored JUD-011 Internal Remedy-Fit review.

**Scope:** Corrected JUD-011 from an opt-in procedural framework into a generally applicable civil anti-nullification cause of action. Replaced the participation matrix with a coverage and independent-alternatives matrix; made JUD-011 alone the preferred remedy for REG-001 and FUND-001; made each subject bill a separate standalone alternative; and removed DOJ-007's incompatible appointments integration.

**Internal Remedy-Fit finding:** JUD-011 itself now defines the actionable executive conduct, statutory-mandate requirement, substantial-and-sustained nullification threshold, eligible-plaintiff categories, Article III limits, sovereign-immunity waiver, D.D.C. procedure, relief, appeal, administration, and application to ongoing violations of mandates enacted before, on, or after JUD-011. It is subject-matter neutral but not a universal public-law cause of action.

**Coverage finding:** REG-001 and FUND-001 are the initial issue applications because both allege substantial executive nullification of enacted institutions, appropriations, or statutory programs. DOJ-007 is excluded because appointment and criminal investigation require distinct constitutional authority. ELEC-001, ELEC-006, ELEC-012, and EMOL-015 remain excluded on their current theories because urgency, national uniformity, election-specific administration, or institutional litigation conflict does not independently satisfy the anti-nullification threshold.

**Presentation finding:** REG-001 and FUND-001 now state two independent congressional choices: enact JUD-011 alone as the preferred general remedy, or enact the subject-specific standalone bill alone. Neither subject bill invokes or activates JUD-011. DOJ-007 now states that it is outside the framework.

**Score effect:** No score assigned. The project-level consistency requirement and JUD-011 change marker are resolved, but JUD-011 remains a fixed-zero candidate pending a T1 current-status, legal-fit, prior-proposal, standing, assignment, appellate, implementation, budget, and quality audit.

### 2026-07-13 — Initial issue admission and working-draft creation

**Audit status:** Candidate working draft created; no scored audit run.

**Scope:** Admitted the absence of a reusable interbranch-review pathway as a distinct A-04 candidate and created an initial D.D.C.-based Article III framework. The draft preserves substantive ownership in participating proposals, requires express statutory participation, reaches continuing violations of older underlying mandates through newly enacted remedial statutes, and requires independently operative fallback fora.

**Initial coordination finding:** REG-001 and FUND-001 are the strongest initial Civil Compliance candidates. DOJ-007 may share administration only through a separate appointments division, distinct judges, decisional and informational firewalls, and its existing constitutional dependency. JUD-010 remains the capacity and appropriations companion. JUD-001 and ordinary issuing-court contempt matters are excluded by default.

**Budget finding:** New budget authority is likely. The working legislation uses a provisional startup and multi-year operating appropriation plus two additional D.D.C. judgeships. CBO's H.R. 1702 judgeship estimate is an initial comparator only; AOUSC, CBO, and legislative-counsel review remain required.

**Change Audit status:** Superseded by the later July 13 project-level integration Change Audit. At this initial stage, the convention remained non-governing and developed proposals retained their prior text.
