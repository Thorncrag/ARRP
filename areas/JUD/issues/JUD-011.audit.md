**Current-status, mootness, and pending-judicial-vulnerability finding:** No current statute or controlling decision makes the proposal moot. *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump* and *Trump v. Circuit merits matters involving impoundment, agency dismantling, officer removal, reviewability, and executive compliance immediately before circulation.

**Standing, jurisdiction, and cause-of-action finding:** The general public, ideological plaintiffs, and individual legislators with only generalized institutional objections remain outside the bill. *U.S. House of Representatives v. Mnuchin* supports standing for a chamber alleging specific impairment of its appropriations power, and *Committee on the Judiciary v. Section 2303 supplies that cause, section 2302(g) supplies original and exclusive D.D.C. jurisdiction, and section 2303(d) preserves every Article III and justiciability requirement. **Sovereign-immunity and defendants finding:** The non-damages waiver tracks the core structure of 5 U.S.C. § 702 but extends to the United States, agencies, and officials covered by JUD-011's independent cause. Johnson*, *Swan v. Clinton*, nonstatutory ultra vires review, mandamus, and any statute-specific exclusive-review provisions.

**Judicial-assignment and panel finding:** Sections 291 and 292 ordinarily use request, public-interest, or certificate mechanisms; section 295 requires source-circuit consent for active judges and public filing of assignments; section 296 supplies assigned judges' powers. Section 2284 authorizes Congress to require a three-judge district court but uses the circuit chief for ordinary designations. FISA, the alien-terrorist removal court, and the Judicial Panel on Multidistrict Litigation demonstrate that Congress may create national Article III rosters or panels with direct statutory designation rules. T2 revised section 2304 to make JUD-011 selections directly authorized rather than dependent on a case-specific request or certificate while preserving section 295 consent and section 296 powers where consistent. T3 must conduct a rule-by-rule check of Rules 12, 23, 24, 41, 42, 53, 65, and 71; sections 1291, 1292, 1404, 1406, 1631, 1651, and 1657; appellate stay and mandamus practice; and D.D.C. and D.C.
