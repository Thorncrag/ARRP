# DOJ-007 — Special-Counsel Dependence and Removal Vulnerability

## Institutional Anomaly

Federal law provides no durable mechanism by which an institution outside the presidential chain of command can initiate and appoint an independent special prosecutor when the President, Attorney General, or senior executive officials possess a disabling conflict of interest. Existing special-counsel arrangements remain dependent on executive initiation, executive jurisdictional decisions, executive resources, and executive removal authority.

## Manifestation of the Failure

When alleged federal criminal conduct concerns the President, presidential associates, senior White House officials, or Department of Justice leadership, the officials ordinarily responsible for deciding whether an independent investigation should exist may themselves be politically, institutionally, or personally conflicted. A loyal Attorney General can prevent appointment, restrict jurisdiction, withhold resources, remove the prosecutor, or terminate the investigation.

Judicial findings that presidential or executive action was unlawful may create substantial grounds for further institutional inquiry, including possible impeachment inquiry or criminal investigation. Such a finding does not automatically establish criminal liability or an impeachable offense. The structural defect arises when credible evidence warrants independent examination but the President controls the institution that would ordinarily decide whether that examination occurs.

## Resulting Damage

The arrangement permits presidential self-protection, retaliatory or selective enforcement, suppression of politically sensitive investigations, removal of prosecutors who resist improper pressure, and loss of public confidence that federal criminal law applies equally to those controlling the executive branch.

## Underlying Weakness

The Appointments Clause permits Congress to vest appointment of inferior officers in courts of law, and *Morrison v. Olson* upheld a judicially appointed independent counsel whose appointment followed an executive request and whose jurisdiction was limited. That precedent does not securely authorize a court to initiate and appoint a prosecutor over executive objection. More recent doctrine, particularly *Trump v. United States*, treats presidential control over Department of Justice investigation and prosecution as part of the President’s exclusive constitutional authority. An ordinary statute authorizing direct judicial initiation would therefore be vulnerable to invalidation.

## Repair and Prevention

The Constitution should authorize Congress to establish a narrowly bounded judicial mechanism for appointing an independent special prosecutor when credible evidence and a structural executive conflict make ordinary Department control unreliable. The judiciary’s role should be confined to determining whether the constitutional and statutory trigger is satisfied, appointing the prosecutor, defining or approving jurisdiction, reviewing removal, and determining when the assignment is complete. The appointing judges should not supervise investigative tactics, charging decisions, plea negotiations, trial strategy, or sentencing positions and should be disqualified from hearing resulting criminal cases.

The constitutional and statutory scheme should expressly authorize the Special Prosecutor to investigate a sitting President, collect and preserve evidence, use grand-jury process, litigate privilege and access disputes, investigate obstruction or retaliation, and transmit substantial and credible information to the House of Representatives. Whether indictment or trial may proceed during the presidential term should remain a separately stated constitutional question and should not be conflated with the authority to investigate.

The mechanism should remain an alternative method of appointing a prosecutor when the executive branch is structurally conflicted. It should not displace impeachment, alter the House’s exclusive authority to impeach, alter the Senate’s authority to try impeachments, or convert the judiciary or prosecutor into an impeachment decisionmaker.

## Least-Complex Adequate Remedy

Adopt a constitutional amendment authorizing Congress to permit a specially constituted federal judicial panel to appoint an independent special prosecutor upon a verified petition and a finding that:

1. credible information indicates that a federal offense or obstruction may have occurred; and
2. a conflict involving the President, Vice President, Attorney General, senior White House officials, presidential campaign activity, or Department leadership materially impairs the reliability of ordinary executive investigation or prosecution.

The amendment should expressly remove qualifying appointments and resulting prosecutorial decisions from presidential direction and direct removal while preserving congressional definition of crimes and procedure, judicial protection of defendants’ rights, lawful oversight, professional discipline, transparent reporting, and removal for defined misconduct or incapacity.

## Proposed Constitutional Amendment

> **Section 1.** Congress may by law authorize a court of the United States, or a judicial panel established by law, to appoint an independent special prosecutor when credible information and a conflict of interest materially impair the ordinary investigation or prosecution of an offense against the United States.
>
> **Section 2.** A special prosecutor appointed under this article shall exercise the investigative and prosecutorial authority prescribed by law and shall not be subject to direction, supervision, or removal by the President or by any officer acting under presidential authority, except as this article or implementing law may provide consistently with the independence of the office. The authority conferred may include investigation of a sitting President, compulsory process, preservation of evidence, and transmission of substantial and credible information to the House of Representatives.
>
> **Section 3.** Congress shall provide by law for the jurisdiction, qualifications, tenure, resources, reporting duties, removal standards, judicial review, and termination of an appointment under this article. Removal shall require defined cause and written findings and shall be subject to expedited judicial review.
>
> **Section 4.** The appointing court or panel shall not direct investigative or prosecutorial decisions and shall not participate in any criminal case arising from an appointment made under this article.
>
> **Section 5.** Nothing in this article shall diminish the authority of Congress to define offenses and criminal procedure, the authority of courts to protect the rights of accused persons, or lawful legislative oversight that does not direct the disposition of a particular criminal matter. Nothing in this article shall displace or bypass the constitutional impeachment process or alter the respective impeachment powers of the House of Representatives and the Senate.
>
> **Section 6.** Congress shall have power to enforce and implement this article by appropriate legislation.

## Enabling Legislation

The implementing proposal is maintained separately as the [Independent Special Prosecutor Appointment and Accountability Act](../../../legislation/DOJ-007.md). It establishes the Special Division, authorized petitioners, judicial referrals, appointment standards, prosecutorial powers, investigative access, removal protections, funding, reporting, impeachment-information transmission, and termination procedures that would take effect following ratification of the amendment.

## Design Requirements for Implementing Legislation

Implementing legislation should provide:

- a three-judge appellate panel selected by rotation or random assignment from different circuits;
- verified petitions from specifically authorized institutional actors rather than unrestricted private applications;
- judicial referral where a court has found executive conduct unlawful and the record also contains credible evidence of a possible federal offense and an executive conflict;
- a threshold inquiry limited to credible evidence and structural conflict, not guilt, probable cause, or an impeachment determination;
- appointment through a merit-based process;
- defined initial jurisdiction and judicial approval of material expansion;
- express authority to investigate a sitting President, preserve evidence, use grand-jury process, and transmit substantial and credible information to the House;
- compliance with generally applicable Department of Justice internal rules and policies, subject to notice-only departure for good cause stated in writing to the Special Division;
- no requirement that the Special Prosecutor obtain permission from the Special Division before departing from a nonbinding Department rule or policy;
- independent minimum investigative capacity and binding access to relevant federal investigative resources;
- protected funding sufficient to prevent executive strangulation of the investigation;
- removal only for misconduct, incapacity, substantial dereliction, or material jurisdictional violation;
- written removal findings and expedited judicial review;
- transmission of substantial and credible impeachment-related information to the House Judiciary Committee without transferring impeachment judgment to the prosecutor or judiciary;
- an express statement that the mechanism supplements rather than bypasses impeachment and provides only an alternative means of appointment outside the executive branch;
- final public reporting consistent with grand-jury secrecy, classified-information protection, defendants’ rights, and legitimate privacy interests;
- mandatory disqualification of appointing judges from resulting prosecutions; and
- termination when the assigned matter has been completed.

## Annotation

**Basis and Evidence.** *Morrison v. Olson*, 487 U.S. 654 (1988), sustained judicial appointment of a limited inferior officer under the former independent-counsel statute, but the Attorney General controlled the statutory trigger. *Trump v. United States*, 603 U.S. 593 (2024), treated presidential control of executive investigation and prosecution as exclusive Article II authority, making a direct judicial trigger constitutionally insecure without amendment. The proposed amendment therefore changes the constitutional allocation explicitly rather than relying on an unstable statutory inference. See [*Trump v. United States*](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/23pdf/603us1r57_6k47.pdf) and [*Morrison v. Olson*](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/487/654/).

**Qualification.** Judicial appointment is not judicial prosecution. An adverse judicial ruling may justify referral, but unlawfulness does not itself establish a federal crime, corrupt intent, or an impeachable offense. The amendment and implementing statute preserve strict separation between the panel’s institutional appointment function, the prosecutor’s independent case decisions, and the House’s exclusive impeachment judgment. Independence from presidential control also must not become independence from law, courts, congressional legislation, professional discipline, transparent standards, or removal for genuine misconduct.

A Special Prosecutor should ordinarily follow Department of Justice internal rules and policies to preserve consistency, fairness, and institutional legitimacy. Because a conflicted executive branch could use internal rules to frustrate the appointment, however, departure should require good cause and written notification—not advance judicial or executive permission. The panel may receive and preserve the notice but should not become the prosecutor’s policy supervisor.

**Remedial Alternatives and Constraints.** A statutory restoration of the former *Morrison*-style mechanism would be simpler but would leave the Attorney General capable of blocking the trigger. Judicial review of an Attorney General’s refusal would reduce that vulnerability but would remain constitutionally contestable. A permanent independent federal prosecution authority would provide broader insulation but would be substantially more complex and disruptive. The amendment proposed here is narrower: it creates an exceptional conflict mechanism while leaving ordinary federal prosecution within the executive branch and preserving impeachment as the constitutional mechanism for removal from office.
