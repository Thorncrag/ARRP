# IMM-001 — Criminal Immunity for Presidential Official Acts

## Institutional Anomaly

Current doctrine grants a former President absolute immunity from criminal prosecution for conduct within the President’s conclusive and preclusive constitutional authority and at least presumptive immunity for other official acts. It also restricts examination of motive and the evidentiary use of protected official conduct. The result may prevent otherwise valid criminal law from reaching conduct undertaken through official authority.

## Manifestation of the Failure

In *Trump v. United States*, 603 U.S. 593 (2024), the Supreme Court held that presidential communications with DOJ concerning investigation and prosecution fall within the President’s exclusive constitutional authority and therefore receive absolute criminal immunity. The Court further held that a former President receives at least presumptive immunity for other official acts and generally prohibited inquiry into motive when classifying conduct.

The decision insulated alleged efforts to use DOJ authority to promote sham or pretextual investigations from criminal adjudication, even though the same factual conduct may remain relevant to impeachment, congressional oversight, institutional reform, and the liability of other participants.

## Resulting Damage

The doctrine can place criminal use of official power beyond ordinary judicial accountability, exclude highly probative evidence, reward the use of governmental machinery rather than private means, delay adjudication, and create uncertainty over whether generally applicable criminal statutes constrain presidential conduct.

## Underlying Weakness

The Constitution contains no express presidential criminal-immunity clause. The Court derived immunity from separation-of-powers concerns and the need to avoid chilling lawful presidential decisionmaking. Those concerns are legitimate but need not require categorical immunity, exclusion of official-act evidence, or a bar on examining motive where criminal intent is an element.

The principal problem is not that every mistaken or abusive policy decision should become criminal. Criminal liability must still depend on a valid statute, all offense elements, proof beyond a reasonable doubt, and ordinary constitutional protections.

## Repair and Prevention

The Constitution should clarify that official status alone does not create immunity from criminal investigation, indictment, prosecution, conviction, or punishment. It should permit examination of motive where intent is legally relevant and permit evidence of official conduct subject to ordinary evidentiary, privilege, and constitutional rules.

The amendment should preserve lawful discretion by requiring that prosecution rest on a generally applicable criminal law and proof of every element. It should not criminalize policy disagreement, good-faith constitutional interpretation, or the lawful exercise of presidential authority standing alone.

## Least-Complex Adequate Remedy

Adopt a constitutional amendment providing that:

1. no President or former President is immune from criminal accountability merely because conduct was official;
2. use of a core presidential power may constitute evidence or an element of a generally applicable offense where all constitutional and statutory requirements are satisfied;
3. motive may be considered where intent is an offense element;
4. official-act evidence is admissible subject to ordinary law;
5. impeachment and Senate conviction are not prerequisites to criminal prosecution;
6. a sitting President may remain temporarily protected from indictment or trial while in office, with limitations periods tolled; and
7. independent screening and expedited review protect against partisan or legally deficient prosecutions.

## Proposed Constitutional Amendment

The proposed text is maintained separately as the [Presidential Criminal Accountability Amendment](../../../legislation/IMM-001.md).

## Relationship to DOJ-007

[DOJ-007](../../a-01-department-of-justice/issues/DOJ-007.md) owns the institutional mechanism for independent investigation, evidence preservation, prosecution of other persons, and reporting to Congress when ordinary executive investigation is unreliable.

IMM-001 owns the substantive rule governing whether a President or former President may claim criminal immunity. The two proposals are independently operable but mutually reinforcing.

## Annotation

**Basis and Evidence.** *Trump v. United States* recognized absolute immunity for core presidential powers, presumptive immunity for other official acts, and no immunity for unofficial acts. It also limited motive inquiry and use of immune official-act evidence.

**Qualification.** The amendment should not authorize prosecution based solely on controversial policy, lawful use of a constitutional power, or disagreement with a predecessor administration. All offense elements, burdens of proof, privileges, and defendant protections remain applicable.

**Remedial Alternatives and Constraints.** Ordinary legislation may clarify statutory coverage but cannot reliably supersede a constitutional immunity rule announced by the Supreme Court. A constitutional amendment is therefore the least-complex durable remedy.
