# DOJ-002 Evidence Record

This page supplements [DOJ-002 — White House Direction or Interference in Particular Federal Criminal Matters](../issues/DOJ-002.md). The issue page remains authoritative for the diagnosis, remedy, proposed Justice Manual provision, score, and conclusions.

## Record Scope

This record preserves verified evidence and counterevidence concerning possible political direction, review, or retaliation in particular federal investigative matters. It distinguishes allegations and threatened action from findings based on completed discovery or adjudication.

## Judicial and Official Dispositions

### Review of personnel associated with January 6 and classified-documents investigations

In *Does 1–9 v. Department of Justice*, plaintiffs challenged a requested collection of information concerning FBI personnel who had worked on January 6 and classified-documents investigations. After jurisdictional discovery, the District Court for the District of Columbia [dismissed the action](https://docs.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/district-of-columbia/dcdce/1%3A2025cv00325/277085/54) because the record did not show that disclosure or adverse employment action was imminent. The opinion therefore does not establish that retaliatory action occurred. It also does not decide whether a later concrete disclosure, reassignment, termination, or politically directed investigative step would be lawful.

## Evidentiary Significance

The decision is counterevidence against presenting the requested personnel review itself as a completed retaliatory manifestation. It remains relevant to DOJ-002 because the litigation shows how political-direction concerns may arise before an overt investigative or personnel action becomes sufficiently concrete for judicial review. DOJ-003 and DOJ-007 may use the same record only as a boundary or comparator; it is not proof of selective prosecution or a predicate for independent counsel.

## Monitoring Items

- Renew review only if a later official record, final personnel action, disclosure, Inspector General finding, congressional investigation, or judicial decision supplies concrete facts not present in the dismissal record.
