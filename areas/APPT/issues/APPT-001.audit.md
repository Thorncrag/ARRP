# APPT-001 — Audit History

This file preserves the full internal review history for [APPT-001](APPT-001.md). The issue page keeps only the compact Proposal Scoring summary.

## Audit History

### 2026-07-16 — Candidate consolidation and initial statutory draft

**Audit status:** In development; T1 audit not yet completed

**Proposal-quality score:** 0

**Audit scope:** Issue-admission analysis, political-failure boundary review, reciprocal-design review, consolidation of eight provisional APPT candidates, preliminary-candidate filtering, HOR-028 integration, source development, and creation of an initial statutory vehicle. This was not a T0–T4 quality audit and does not increment `Runs`.

**Disposition:** APPT-001 is retained and retitled as the primary home for temporary-service, vacancy, delegation, succession, transparency, and enforcement mechanisms that can substitute for confirmation or defeat a statutory appointment backstop. APPT-002, APPT-003, APPT-007, and APPT-008 are merged into APPT-001. The authority-manipulation portion of APPT-006 is merged into APPT-001; misleading public terminology remains with FACT-009. APPT-005 is divided among APPT-001, REG-001, and ordinary political removal outside scope.

**Separate candidate preserved:** APPT-004 is reframed as exercise of governmental authority by unappointed or insufficiently accountable individuals. HOR-028 and preliminary intake question INTAKE-GAP-004 are incorporated into that source-development lane rather than creating a third APPT proposal.

**Institutional boundary:** Appointment reform is not justified merely because Congress declines oversight, a nominee is controversial, an acting officer implements objectionable policy, or a later election may change control. The retained defect is excessive reliance on delayed political or judicial correction where temporary or ambiguously assigned authority can cause difficult-to-repair individual harm, degrade democratic processes, remove a safeguard against consequential abuse, or establish a repeatable confirmation-evasion method.

**Reciprocal-design finding:** The proposal must operate identically when party control is reversed. It must address an aligned congressional majority's passive acquiescence without giving an opposition Senate a mechanism to disable ordinary government operations. The initial direction therefore preserves reasonable temporary service, lawful career succession, discrete task delegation, and third-party reliance while making substantially complete delegation, expired service, and succession-backstop manipulation visible and reviewable.

**Existing-mechanism finding:** Congress has already created the principal machinery through the Federal Vacancies Reform Act, GAO vacancy review, the PLUM Reporting website, office-specific succession statutes, and ordinary judicial review. The initial proposal strengthens those mechanisms rather than creating a new commission, tribunal, or judgeship.

**Initial draft elements:** An anti-evasion rule for substantially complete delegation; written and public delegation notices; prompt agency reporting; GAO compliance review; PLUM data integration; proportional no-force-or-effect consequences; expedited subject-specific judicial review; preservation of the section 546(d) judicial appointment backstop after presidential removal; implementation and transition provisions; and severability.

**Unresolved findings:** T1 must test the definition of substantially complete authority; the line between lawful delegation and acting service; constitutional limits under the Appointments Clause and presidential removal doctrine; interaction with agency-specific succession laws; the treatment of agency heads and first assistants; the scope and timing of GAO determinations; third-party reliance and ratification; Article III standing; appropriate venue and relief; classified or privileged records; the section 546 transition; and whether GAO and OPM require a specific authorization of appropriations.

**Score and lifecycle effect:** No formula-based score was assigned and no T-audit run was added. APPT-001 and APPT-004 move from pending development to active development. APPT-001 remains at fixed score zero until a completed score-bearing audit.

**Next audit need:** Complete statutory and historical source development, then conduct a T1 review of issue identity, legal durability, enactment pathway, evidence, abuse resistance, drafting quality, fiscal posture, and proposal-to-legislation consistency.
