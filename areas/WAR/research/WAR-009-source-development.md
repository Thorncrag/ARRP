# WAR-009 Source Development — Executive Lethal Targeting and Accountability

## Purpose

This record develops and verifies the evidence, legal baselines, comparators, remedy models, and monitoring predicates supporting [WAR-009](../issues/WAR-009.md). It separates authorization questions owned by [WAR-001](../issues/WAR-001.md) from target identification, legal classification, procedural safeguards, civilian protection, and accountability owned by WAR-009.

## Principal Manifestations

### Alleged drug-trafficking vessel strikes

- **Official strike description.** U.S. Southern Command reported three February 16, 2026 strikes on vessels it assessed were engaged in narco-trafficking and operated by designated terrorist organizations, killing eleven people. The release identifies an intelligence assessment but does not publish target-specific evidence. See `SRC-2633`.
- **Law-enforcement comparator.** An August 2025 Navy–Coast Guard operation used warning shots, disabling fire, boarding, field testing, seizure, and arrest without reported injury. It demonstrates an established contemporaneous interdiction model, not that interdiction was feasible in every later encounter. See `SRC-2634`.
- **Congressional information request.** A bipartisan November 2025 House letter requested the legal authority, positive-identification process, alternatives analysis, post-strike identity verification, and review mechanisms. See `SRC-2635`.
- **Scale and public-evidence limits.** Reuters reported forty-five strikes and 157 deaths by March 17, 2026 while describing the limited target-specific evidence released publicly. See `SRC-2636`.
- **Litigation.** *Burnley v. United States* contests an October 2025 strike. The docket remains pending and supplies allegations rather than adjudicated facts. See `SRC-0998`.

### Iranian infrastructure threats

- **Current one-for-one threat.** Associated Press reporting documents President Trump's July 22, 2026 statement that each Iranian attack on a vessel in the Strait of Hormuz would produce destruction of one bridge or power plant. See `SRC-2637`.
- **Official prior statement.** A March 23 official transcript records the President saying that destruction of Iran's largest electric-generating facilities was expected the next morning and describing the threatened operation as negotiating leverage. See `SRC-2638`.
- **Civilian-harm capacity.** A May 2026 Defense Department Inspector General evaluation found that no Civilian Harm Mitigation and Response Action Plan objective was fully implemented by fiscal year 2025 and identified reversals involving personnel, funding, tools, and institutional capacity. See `SRC-2639`.

## Statutory and Operational Baseline

- `SRC-2640` — 10 U.S.C. § 124: Defense Department detection and monitoring in support of counterdrug law enforcement.
- `SRC-2641` — 10 U.S.C. § 284: authorized counterdrug and transnational-organized-crime support.
- `SRC-2642` — 10 U.S.C. § 275: restriction on direct military participation in search, seizure, arrest, or similar activity absent separate authority.
- `SRC-2643` — 14 U.S.C. § 522: Coast Guard high-seas inquiry, search, seizure, and arrest authority.
- `SRC-2644` — 18 U.S.C. § 2441: present federal War Crimes Act coverage and jurisdiction.
- `SRC-2645` — DoD Directive 2311.01: law-of-war compliance, advice, reporting, investigation, and corrective-action framework.
- `SRC-2646` — Constitution Annotated: the House's sole impeachment power and the historically developed, nonstatutory character of the high-Crimes-and-Misdemeanors standard.

## Proposition and Qualification Matrix

| Question | Current support | Qualification or next step |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Did Congress create a military combat authority for ordinary drug interdiction? | Sections 124, 275, and 284 describe detection, monitoring, and support to law enforcement. | Complete a statute-by-statute search for any later or operation-specific lethal-force authority before finalizing the categorical conclusion. |
| Were target identities and evidence publicly substantiated? | Official releases generally state intelligence conclusions; Congress requested positive-identification and review details. | Classified evidence may exist. The defect is insufficient independent review and public or congressional accountability, not proof that no evidence exists. |
| Was interdiction available? | A contemporaneous Navy–Coast Guard operation demonstrates a functioning model. | Feasibility must be evaluated operation by operation; do not infer that every struck vessel could have been safely boarded. |
| Do infrastructure threats establish a war crime? | Statements show category-based retaliatory and coercive language. | A threat is not a completed strike, and dual-use target legality depends on operational facts, military advantage, proportionality, and precautions. |
| Does the Fifth Amendment govern every victim? | Extraterritorial constitutional coverage is contested and fact dependent. | WAR-009 creates statutory safeguards without resolving the constitutional minimum. |
| Can a statute define an impeachable offense conclusively? | Congress may state its institutional understanding and create referral procedures. | It cannot bind the House's sole impeachment judgment or the Senate's sole trial judgment; a future chamber may alter a rulemaking provision. |

1. a merits, appellate, or final disposition in *Burnley*;

3. a final Defense Department Inspector General targeting-cycle evaluation;
4. additional official casualty, survivor, identification, or rescue disclosures;
5. congressional authorization, prohibition, investigation, or public findings concerning the vessel campaign;
6. evidence establishing the status and effects of reported Iranian infrastructure attacks; and
7. material changes to the War Crimes Act, UCMJ, civilian-harm framework, or executive targeting policy.
