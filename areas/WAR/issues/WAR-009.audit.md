### 2026-07-22 — Issue admission and initial proposal development

**Audit status:** Initial defined-proposal package complete; first internal audit pending; no proposal-quality score assigned

**Proposal-quality score:** 0

**Runs:** No change. Issue admission, source development, and initial drafting are not T0–T4 issue-quality audits.

**Admission finding:** The approved issue identifies a defect distinct from WAR-001. WAR-001 owns congressional authorization and continuation of hostilities. WAR-009 owns the executive process for classifying a planned lethal operation, substantiating individual or object targetability, distinguishing armed conflict from law enforcement, preserving evidence, identifying victims, and providing independent review and accountability.

**Manifestation finding:** The alleged drug-vessel campaign supplies the principal manifestation because the executive reportedly converted suspected criminal trafficking into deliberate military targeting while withholding much of the target-specific evidence and legal criteria. Repeated threats against categories of Iranian civilian or dual-use infrastructure supply a second manifestation of the danger that retaliatory policy may obscure individualized law-of-war review. Neither manifestation is treated as a final judicial finding that every strike was unlawful.

**Human-governed foundation:** On July 22, 2026, the human author approved the institutional failure, the principal manifestations, a statutory law-enforcement/armed-conflict/no-basis fork with minimum procedural and accountability safeguards, and a federal legislative vehicle. The author also approved a component declaring knowing presidential war crimes properly cognizable as impeachable abuse of office while preserving the House's and Senate's constitutional judgments.

**Initial remedy finding:** One severable bill should regulate planned extraterritorial lethal operations under U.S. authority regardless of the executing agency. It should make law enforcement the default for suspected crime, require specific congressional authority for combat targeting, preserve actual emergency self-defense, prevent designation alone from creating targetability, require records and independent review, and create criminal and congressional referrals.

**Unresolved finding:** The first development audit must test the bill against Article II command authority, Congress's Article I powers, *Trump v. United States*, existing counterdrug and Coast Guard statutes, the War Crimes Act, UCMJ jurisdiction, international armed-conflict and noninternational-armed-conflict thresholds, civilian-harm policy, inspector-general authorities, classified-information protection, judicial review, House rulemaking, operational feasibility, and appropriations.

**Closeout status:** The initial issue page, source basis, and concrete federal legislative vehicle now perform the functions required for a productive first audit. The proposal therefore advances from `In development` to `Audit needed` without a score or audit-run increment.

**Next review need:** Conduct a T0 triage scan, then complete statute-first, constitutional, operational, fiscal, and stakeholder review and reconcile the initial bill with WAR-001 and DOJ-007 before any score-bearing audit.
