## Audit History

### 2026-07-15 — Initial source-development pass

**Audit status:** Initial source development complete; remedy and legislation pending

**Proposal-quality score:** 0 (Not Scored)

**Audit scope:** Initial promotion from an area-only candidate to a standalone source-development page; institutional-boundary analysis; review of the FY2026 Homeland Security Grant Program notice; identification of sanctuary-cooperation and election-administration manifestations; preliminary constitutional and statutory fit; and adjacent-issue routing.

**Material findings:** The FY2026 HSGP notice places both manifestations within a single grant-administration mechanism. It requires a border-enforcement investment and minimum expenditure while separately withholding 20 percent of the total HSGP award pending compliance with election-administration requirements. The official program listing identifies HSGP as a $1.064 billion terrorism and catastrophic-event preparedness program. Executive Order 14287 separately directs agencies to identify grants and contracts for suspension or termination based on sanctuary designation.

The issue is institutional rather than a judgment on sanctuary or election policy. Election systems can be legitimate homeland-security subjects, and Congress may impose immigration- or election-related conditions within constitutional limits. The identified defect is executive use of funding leverage to obtain broader State policy changes without a clearly identified condition enacted by Congress.

**Statutory and doctrinal fit reviewed:** 6 U.S.C. §§ 603 and 605; Executive Order 14287; the FY2026 HSGP opportunity listing and notice; *South Dakota v. Dole*; *NFIB v. Sebelius*; *Murphy v. NCAA*; and a D.R.I. order enforcing a preliminary injunction against a FEMA review process using sanctuary-jurisdiction restrictions.

**Overlap findings:** FED-003 owns the grant-condition mechanism. FED-006 remains the home for direct interference with State election systems. FUND-004 owns selective or retaliatory withholding as fiscal administration, while FUND-001 owns nonobligation contrary to an enacted appropriation or mandate. RIGHTS-004 owns substantive immigration and due-process questions.

**Unresolved findings:** The next pass must identify the full program authorization and appropriations history, determine whether any provision expressly authorizes each challenged condition, review current litigation and any final merits rulings, compare prior HSGP conditions, select the least-complex statutory home, define covered cross-policy conditions, determine the appropriate plaintiff and forum, test relief for existing awards, and prepare a preliminary budget classification.
