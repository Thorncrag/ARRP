**HAVA and EAC finding:** HAVA remains a plausible amendment hook for a federal access form, state-publication interface, guidance, technical assistance, grants, complaint routing, and public certification-status reporting. **FECA and FEC enforcement finding:** FECA and 11 C.F.R. § 110.13 remain the natural debate-transparency hook because the existing regulation already recognizes candidate-debate treatment, staging organizations, at least two candidates, no promotion or advancement of one candidate over another, and pre-established objective selection criteria. T2 also confirms that 52 U.S.C. § 30109 is a real implementation weakness: reason-to-believe findings, probable-cause findings, civil actions, and many enforcement steps require four affirmative FEC votes, while private judicial review is largely routed through dismissal or failure-to-act procedures. The draft's deadlock-and-delay clause is directionally useful but not yet a complete debate-cycle remedy.

**Presidential-versus-congressional scope finding:** The congressional-election interface has the stronger Elections Clause footing. The presidential-election provisions should remain staged and cautious: filing-interface, publication, certification-status, public-benefit, and transparency rules are more defensible than a direct national ballot-entitlement command. Article II elector-appointment limits, state certification timing, and late-litigation risk remain T3 blockers before expanding the presidential-access provisions.

**Debate-gatekeeping and current-status finding:** The 2024 CNN/RFK Jr. dispute remains a useful modern comparator because it shows how polling thresholds, state-certified ballot access, claimed-versus-confirmed access, and debate timing can interact. AP reporting on Kennedy's FEC complaint and later exclusion supports the source-development point that debate criteria can generate FEC-facing controversy, but T2 does not treat CNN's criteria as unlawful or treat the complaint as proven. Official sponsor criteria, FEC complaint records, advisory opinions, enforcement matters, and any final agency or court disposition remain unresolved.
