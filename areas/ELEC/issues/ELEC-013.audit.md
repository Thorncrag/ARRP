ELEC-013's next draft should focus on debate-transparency and neutrality conditions tied to public benefits, federal-election ballot-access notice and cure floors, FEC administration, and constitutional authority. Created ELEC-015 and moved RCV/majority-choice ownership there.

**Unresolved findings:** The next ELEC-013 pass should draft the federal candidate-access and debate-gatekeeping bill; source-develop FEC advisory opinions and enforcement matters; verify official debate sponsor criteria and litigation; map Elections Clause, Article II, Spending Clause, HAVA, First Amendment, press-freedom, party-association, and Anderson/Burdick limits; review state ballot-access statutes and cure procedures; and develop implementation-cost analogues.

**Audit process feedback:** This entry should be treated as a boundary-correction pass. It does not replace the no-draft preflight rule for later audits: unless a draft is added first, future ELEC-013 audits should notify the user that the issue remains fixed-zero and candidate-only.

**Remedy-selection finding:** T2 selects a staged federal-first election-competition package with a companion model-state and pilot-grant track. The adequate first vehicle should focus on debate-transparency conditions tied to public benefits, federal-election ballot-access notice-and-cure floors, voluntary state pilot grants, implementation guardrails, voter education, accessibility, tabulation transparency, recount/audit procedures, cost reporting, and a limited study-and-report component for larger structural reforms.

**Rejected first-vehicle options:** T2 rejects an immediate national ranked-choice-voting mandate, immediate federal open-primary mandate, freestanding fusion entitlement, proportional-representation rewrite, Fair Representation Act-style multi-member congressional overhaul, and pure study commission as the first ELEC-013 vehicle. Those options remain comparators, reserve components, or later companion proposals. H.R. 4632 is especially useful as a broad comparator but too sweeping for the least-complex first draft because it combines voting-method, district-structure, redistricting, and implementation-payment reforms.

**Prior-proposal comparison:** H.R. 4632 confirms that a current federal proposal exists for congressional RCV, multi-member districts, at-large fallback rules, redistricting criteria, voting-rights safeguards, and implementation payments. H.Res. 20 confirms a process model for hearings, expert evidence, study of alternative congressional election methods, and review of federal barriers to state experimentation. T2 uses H.R. 4632 as a substantive comparator and H.Res. 20 as a study/process comparator, not as ready-made ARRP text.

**Source-development finding:** T2 upgraded the source base with GovInfo full-text links for H.R. 4632 and H.Res. 20; official Maine RCV voter-instruction, tabulation, rule, recount, and results/data sources; and NCSL's March 23, 2026 ranked-choice voting state-law map. T2 also added partial primary-source routes for D.C. Council Initiative 83 text, D.C. Board of Elections 2024 general-election results, and Alaska Division of Elections 2024 general-election results. These D.C. and Alaska rows are useful routing sources but remain partial because exact certified result totals, implementation/funding records, Alaska recount records, and 2026 repeal-measure materials still require deeper primary-source review.

**Legal-readiness finding:** The staged remedy reduces constitutional risk compared with a broad national election-method mandate. Debate-access reform should be tied to public benefits, official sponsorship, public-broadcasting support, or disclosure obligations and must avoid compelled private press hosting. Ballot-access floors should be limited to defensible federal-election hooks and tested against the Anderson/Burdick framework, party-association doctrine, Article II, Elections Clause, HAVA, and Spending Clause limits. Fusion and open-primary reforms should remain model-state or pilot options until state-association and federalism risks are mapped more fully.

**Implementation finding:** Maine's official RCV pages and results/data files support the need for voter education, ballot-marking examples, tabulation glossaries, public reports, cast-vote-record or equivalent transparency, and recount rules. NCSL's current state-law map shows both adoption and prohibition trends, so a voluntary-pilot path is more adoption-realistic than immediate national preemption.

**Score effect:** Score remains fixed at 0/100. T2 improved remedy selection and source routing, but ELEC-013 still has no legislative text, model-state act, enforceable policy draft, cost estimate, or final constitutional authority map. No formula-based Proposal Quality Score, Adoption Friction Score, Required Electoral Environment, or Development Priority should be assigned until the first draft exists.
