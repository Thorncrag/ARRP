## Audit History

### 2026-07-03 — Issue Spin-Off / Source-Development Admission

**Audit status:** Pending development

**Scope:** Created ELEC-014 as a distinct A-02 candidate issue after human review concluded that the Ohio Organizing Collaborative FBI-search reporting does not fit ELEC-004's Anti-SLAPP-style civil-procedure remedy. The issue page captures the federal criminal-process concern without drafting a statute or assigning formula-based proposal credit.

**Admission rationale:** The candidate identifies a distinct institutional weakness requiring separate diagnosis and remedial analysis: federal criminal investigative process can chill voting, voter registration, voting-rights work, election administration, and state control of elections if deployed without election-specific predicates, timing safeguards, neutrality review, minimization, and reporting. The issue is adjacent to DOJ politicization proposals, but the protected institutional interest is election participation and state-administered election integrity.

**Source posture:** The Ohio Organizing Collaborative reporting is retained only as a source-development trigger. Current public sources are secondary reporting and criticism. Publication-ready reliance requires primary materials such as warrant records, a Rule 41 docket or return, official DOJ/FBI statements, inspector-general findings, congressional records, or civil litigation records. EO 14399 is retained as a related source-development lead only for the criminal-investigative layer of federal election-control architecture.

**Boundary decision:** ELEC-004 now owns election litigation, civil subpoenas, audits, inspection demands, and comparable noncriminal process. ELEC-014 owns federal criminal searches, criminal subpoenas, grand-jury process, interviews, device seizures, voter-data demands, and other federal criminal investigative actions directed at state-administered elections, voter-registration activity, election officials, ballots, voting systems, or voting-rights organizations.

**Score effect:** Proposal Quality Score is fixed at 0/100 because ELEC-014 is a candidate/source-development issue with no selected least-complex adequate remedy and no proposed legislative vehicle. No adoption, friction, or formula score should be assigned until the issue receives a T1/T2 development pass.

**Next audit need:** Run issue-admission and source-development pass. Prioritize DOJ election-crimes guidance, Justice Manual politically sensitive investigation rules, Public Integrity and Civil Rights Division approval channels, Rule 41, grand-jury process, voter-data and associational-privacy safeguards, federalism limits, state election administration impacts, legitimate federal voting-rights and election-crime enforcement needs, and any primary records arising from the Ohio search.

**Audit process feedback:** The spin-off improves issue-boundary discipline. ELEC-004 should not be stretched to cover criminal investigative process simply because both mechanisms can chill election activity. ELEC-014 needs criminal-procedure and voting-rights source development before drafting.
