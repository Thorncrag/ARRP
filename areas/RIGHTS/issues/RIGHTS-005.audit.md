# RIGHTS-005 — Audit History

This file preserves the full review history for [RIGHTS-005](RIGHTS-005.md). The issue page keeps only the compact Proposal Scoring summary.

## Audit History

### 2026-07-21 — Horizon candidate admission and initial drafting

**Audit status:** In development; no proposal-quality score assigned

**Proposal-quality score:** 0

**Runs:** No change. Horizon admission and initial drafting are not a T0–T4 issue-quality audit.

**Admission finding:** HOR-022 was admitted independently as RIGHTS-005. The institutional defect is not ordinary disagreement with law-enforcement policy or an assertion that federal officers possess general absolute immunity. It is the possibility that qualified immunity, the absence of an express general federal-officer cause of action, restricted *Bivens* remedies, employer substitution and indemnification, the federal criminal mens rea and charging structure, State-federal immunity disputes, and evidence control can combine to leave serious constitutional misconduct without personal civil or criminal accountability.

**Scope finding:** RIGHTS-005 owns law-enforcement civil and criminal accountability. JUD-012 retains the general judicial-review-foreclosure problem; DOM-005 retains comprehensive critical-incident evidence access; DOJ-007 retains independent investigation of presidential and senior executive misconduct; and HOR-045 retains the deferred vehicle-stop safeguard question.

**Initial remedy finding:** One severable bill is preferable to separate civil and criminal proposals because the same constitutional violation may expose gaps in both systems. The initial draft creates an express civil action, removes qualified immunity as a threshold bar, calibrates personal financial responsibility to aggravated findings, amends 18 U.S.C. § 242, preserves valid federal duties and nondiscriminatory State prosecution outside them, retains federal removal, and supplies minimum evidence and charging-review safeguards.

**Unresolved finding:** The personal-contribution formula, abrogation and sovereign-immunity basis, criminal mens rea and fair notice, Supremacy Clause treatment, charging-review independence, evidence overlap with DOM-005, fiscal impact, State implementation evidence, insurance, indemnification, collective bargaining, recruitment, and stakeholder objections require full review before scoring.

**Next review need:** Conduct a statute-first legal-durability review and an empirical source-development pass. Revise the initial bill, complete an implementation and budget estimate, and then run the first score-bearing audit.
