## Audit History

### 2026-07-06 — Post-*Barbara* statutory-vulnerability update

**Audit status:** Post-*Barbara* source-development update complete; no proposal-quality score assigned

**Proposal-quality score:** 0

**Audit scope:** Limited source-development and issue-posture update for RIGHTS-003 after the June 30, 2026 Supreme Court decision in *Trump v. Barbara*. This was not a formula-based proposal-quality audit because RIGHTS-003 still has no drafted legislation or equivalent proposal vehicle.

**Source record reviewed:** The official Supreme Court opinion in *Trump v. Barbara* and the existing RIGHTS-003 source inventory row for that opinion.

**Material findings:** The majority holding means RIGHTS-003 should not frame the core Citizenship Clause question as unresolved. The separate opinion by Justice Kavanaugh, concurring in the judgment and dissenting in part, identifies 8 U.S.C. § 1401(a) as the statutory baseline that the executive order violated and describes statutory amendment as Congress's available route if it wants to change the governing rule. That does not eliminate the majority's constitutional holding, but it does identify a statutory attack surface and records-implementation vulnerability that can support a narrower ARRP proposal.

**Defects corrected:** Updated the issue title, remedy type, institutional anomaly, manifestation narrative, proposal survey, least-complex-remedy note, repair/prevention list, scoring summary, annotation, and source notes so RIGHTS-003 is no longer framed as possibly moot or retirement-first. The issue now points toward a statutory citizenship-recognition and records-continuity backstop.

**Unresolved findings:** No statutory vehicle has been drafted. A later pass should decide whether the bill should directly amend 8 U.S.C. § 1401(a), add a new records-and-review section in title 8, amend passport and Social Security record authorities, or combine those routes.

**Risk notes:** The proposal must not imply Congress can constitutionally overrule the *Barbara* majority by ordinary statute. The legally durable frame is statutory codification, agency implementation, reliance protection, records preservation, transition rules, and expedited review, with constitutional challenge preserved for any attempted rollback.

**Next audit need:** Run a source-development or first-draft pass focused on 8 U.S.C. § 1401(a), related citizenship-recognition statutes, passport and Social Security record authorities, state vital-record reliance, and expedited-review models.

**Audit process feedback:** The fixed-zero posture remains correct because no draft vehicle exists. The prior "possible retirement" framing was too narrow after review of the separate statutory analysis; future predicate checks should review all controlling and separate opinions before deciding that a post-ruling issue is moot.
