# RIGHTS-002 Evidence Record

This page supplements [RIGHTS-002 — Temporary Protected Status, Asylum Access, and Humanitarian Reviewability](../issues/RIGHTS-002.md). The issue page remains authoritative for the diagnosis, remedy, proposed safeguards, score, and conclusions.

## Record Scope

This record preserves verified judicial dispositions relevant to statutory reviewability of humanitarian-status decisions. It distinguishes current authority from vacated opinions and separates immigration-specific review bars from any broader theory of absent judicial review.

## Judicial and Official Dispositions

### Temporary Protected Status termination litigation

In *Ramos v. Wolf*, a Ninth Circuit panel treated 8 U.S.C. § 1254a(b)(5)(A) as barring review of the plaintiffs' statutory challenge to Temporary Protected Status termination decisions while reaching and rejecting the constitutional claim. The panel opinion was later [vacated for rehearing en banc](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca9/18-16981/18-16981-2020-09-14.html), and the litigation later became moot following policy changes.

## Evidentiary Significance

The vacated panel opinion is not current precedential authority. It remains useful for identifying the claim-specific reviewability question that RIGHTS-002 must address: whether the TPS statute leaves an adequate route to test compliance with governing statutory criteria, administrative process, and applicable constitutional protections. It should not be cited as proof that all TPS decisions are unreviewable or that courts never reached the legality of the challenged action.

## Monitoring Items

- Current appellate decisions construing 8 U.S.C. § 1254a(b)(5)(A), including whether particular statutory, procedural, constitutional, or ultra vires claims remain reviewable.
- Final dispositions in current TPS and asylum-access litigation that clarify what evidence, consultation, notice, or judicial remedy existing law requires.
