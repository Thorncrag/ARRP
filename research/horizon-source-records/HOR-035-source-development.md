# HOR-035 — Executive Review Evasion Through Withdrawal, Replacement, Settlement, or Mootness

> **Source-development record:** This record assembles source-specific research routed to the candidate. It is source-development work, not a completed project finding, admission decision, or determination that the described conduct is unlawful or institutionally defective.

Each entry states the proposition presently under review. `Reviewed: No` means that proposition still requires source-level verification or qualitative placement. A monitoring qualification identifies the future development being watched; it does not establish that the source presently supports a final conclusion.

## Current disposition

On July 23, 2026, the human author directed that HOR-035 remain a formal candidate while receiving active, high-priority investigation. The concern is plausible and may be systematically underreported because the relevant events ordinarily appear in court dockets, withdrawal notices, mootness motions, settlements, vacatur requests, agency notices, and later reissuances rather than in general news coverage. That information problem warrants a docket-first investigation; it does not itself establish the recurring institutional defect required for admission.

The candidate therefore remains at `Development level: Candidate` and `Status: Development`. It is not deferred, blocked, or limited to passive monitoring.

## Research question

Determine whether federal executive defendants, across administrations and agencies, repeatedly use withdrawal, replacement, narrowing, settlement, individualized relief, temporary cessation, or government-caused mootness in a manner that:

1. follows completed harm, meaningful litigation exposure, or an adverse judicial development;
2. prevents effective merits review or eliminates a practically important remedy;
3. preserves the disputed legal authority, permits materially similar conduct to recur, or yields a later reissuance;
4. produces an advantage through dismissal, vacatur, fee treatment, or the loss of adverse precedent; and
5. is not adequately addressed by voluntary-cessation doctrine, mootness exceptions, later challenges, fee rules, ordinary judicial discretion, or an existing subject-specific ARRP proposal.

The inquiry is neutral across administrations and does not presume that settlement, rescission, correction, replacement, or voluntary compliance is improper.

## Investigation protocol

- Build the case universe from primary judicial and executive records, using CourtListener/RECAP and official opinions, dockets, government filings, agency notices, and Federal Register materials before relying on secondary reporting.
- Include multiple administrations and agencies so that a change in presidential party or policy preference does not determine the result.
- Search for government motions or stipulations invoking mootness after rescission, replacement, settlement, individualized relief, or cessation; government requests for vacatur after its own action moots review; dismissals following temporary relief or an adverse ruling; judicial refusals to find mootness; and later recurrence or materially similar reissuance.
- For each episode, record the challenged action, procedural posture, government-caused change, court disposition, whether a merits ruling or relief was lost, completed harm, retained authority, recurrence, vacatur, fees, and the adequacy of existing remedies.
- Retain ordinary corrections, effective settlements, merits decisions, preserved later review, and other non-evasive outcomes as negative controls. Do not count the current leads as manifestations merely because they involve withdrawal or mootness.
- Test overlap with JUD-001, JUD-011, JUD-012, REG-006, and subject-specific proposals before recommending an independent issue.

## Admission test

Recommend independent admission only if the completed record establishes:

- multiple independently verified episodes sufficient to show a repeated or egregious federal practice rather than isolated litigation choices;
- a recurring loss of effective review or remedy attributable to the government's litigation-related change;
- retained authority, recurrence, materially similar reissuance, completed unremedied harm, or another concrete institutional consequence;
- an inadequacy in existing doctrine or remedies; and
- a legally available, neutral, cross-cutting remedy that does not require courts to issue advisory opinions and is not better housed in an existing proposal.

If the evidence instead shows that existing doctrine ordinarily preserves effective review or relief, or that the episodes are legitimate corrections without a recurring institutional consequence, retire or narrow the candidate rather than admitting it.
