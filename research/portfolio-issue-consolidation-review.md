# Portfolio Issue-Consolidation Review

## Purpose and Status

This is an issue-admission and consolidation review of every active ARRP proposal area other than A-12, whose APPT candidates were separately adjudicated on July 16, 2026. It tests whether provisional records identify distinct institutional weaknesses requiring separate diagnosis and remedial analysis or instead describe manifestations, safeguards, enforcement provisions, implementation details, or cross-area applications of a broader proposal.

The user approved the first high-confidence implementation batch on July 16, 2026. APPT was adjudicated separately; EMOL, FRB, HER, REC, RET, PAR, and CONG were then consolidated as recommended here. The remaining area recommendations are non-operative until separately approved and synchronized across the repository and GitHub.

## Review Rules

Each record was screened against the following questions:

1. Does it identify a distinct institutional weakness rather than an example, tactic, consequence, safeguard, or remedy component?
2. Would it require materially different legal authority, factual development, legislation, implementing institutions, or abuse safeguards?
3. Would combining it with the proposed receiving issue make the diagnosis or legislation too broad to remain coherent?
4. Is the claimed failure structural and difficult to correct through ordinary elections, oversight, litigation, or administration, rather than solely a political disagreement?
5. Does another ARRP issue already own the same mechanism or remedy?
6. Can one independently enactable proposal contain multiple coordinated titles without creating separate proposal-development obligations?

Developed and scored proposals were presumptively retained. A recommendation affecting one would require a separate substantive Change Audit. Active Horizon records are outside the numerical baseline and remain subject to the Horizon-adjudication process.

## Portfolio Effect

The review baseline contained **198 active proposal records** after the separately approved APPT consolidation. Twenty-seven were Review Ready, leaving 171 active records below that threshold. The first approved implementation batch reduced the active portfolio by 55 records, to **143 active proposals**, with 27 Review Ready and 116 remaining below that threshold. If every still-pending recommendation below were ultimately approved, the active proposal portfolio would decline to approximately **77 proposals**, a total reduction of **121 records** from the review baseline. With the existing 27 Review Ready proposals preserved, approximately **50 proposals** would remain to reach Review Ready.

The 77-proposal figure remains an architectural planning estimate, not an approved denominator. The approved 143-proposal denominator requires approximately **4.83 additional proposals per week** from July 16 through December 31, 2026. If the remaining recommendations were approved, the 50-proposal remainder would require approximately **2.08 proposals per week**. These estimates assume no additional proposals and no later decision to retain a conditionally merged record independently.

## Area Recommendations

### CIV — Civil Service and Professional Administration

- **Retain CIV-001**, retitled around civil-service merit and anti-politicization, and absorb CIV-002, CIV-003, CIV-006, and CIV-007. Reclassification, loyalty-based hiring, retaliatory reassignment, politicized evaluation, and delayed personnel remedies depend substantially on the same Title 5, MSPB, OSC, notice, burden, and remedy framework.
- **Retain CIV-004** for protection against retaliation for refusing unlawful directives; protected refusal and command responsibility differ from ordinary merit-system administration.
- **Retain CIV-005**, retitled around workforce capacity and anti-hollowing, and absorb CIV-008. Loss of technical capacity is the institutional harm; mass reductions are a principal mechanism.
- **Retain CIV-009** for cross-agency operational control by repurposed or irregular units because DOGE-like appointment, delegation, data-access, procurement, and authority questions require a different framework.

**Result:** 9 to 4. **Confidence:** high, except final allocation of CIV-006 remedies between CIV-001 and CIV-004.

### CLASS — Classification, Declassification, and National-Security Information

- **Retain CLASS-001** for presidential classification, declassification, documentation, and custody; absorb CLASS-002, CLASS-003, CLASS-005, and CLASS-007.
- **Retain CLASS-004** for anti-concealment, overclassification, and independent review; absorb CLASS-008, CLASS-009, and CLASS-010.
- **Retain CLASS-006** for protection of sources, methods, and classified information; absorb CLASS-012's retention-and-disclosure accountability component.
- **Retain CLASS-011** for security-clearance integrity and cross-reference RET-001 when a clearance action is retaliatory.

**Result:** 12 to 4. **Confidence:** medium-high; CLASS-003 requires special Restricted Data analysis before integration.

### CONG — Congressional Oversight and Executive Accountability

- **Retain CONG-001**, retitled around congressional information and subpoena enforcement, and absorb CONG-002 through CONG-006. Privilege, testimonial immunity, contempt routing, litigation delay, and expedited review are defenses or enforcement components of the same access dispute.
- **Retain CONG-007** for committee staffing, technical capacity, secure facilities, counsel, and appropriations.
- **Do not retain CONG-008 independently.** Unified-government oversight collapse is principally a political failure. Its repairable automatic-disclosure component is already addressed by JUD-005 and comparable notice mechanisms.

**Result:** 8 to 2. **Confidence:** high.

### DOJ — Department of Justice

Retain DOJ-001 through DOJ-005, DOJ-007, and DOJ-009. Six are Review Ready and DOJ-004 is deliberately paused for merits adjudication. DOJ-006 and DOJ-008 have already been merged. Further consolidation would disturb developed remedies and requires proposal-specific Change Audits.

**Result:** no change. **Confidence:** high.

- **Retain DOM-007** for domestic military intelligence activity.
- **Retain DOM-009** for federal law-enforcement surveillance procurement and deployment.

**Result:** 9 to 4. **Confidence:** high.

### ELEC — Election Administration, Integrity, and Democratic Participation

### RIGHTS — Civil Rights Administration and Identity Records

- **Retain RIGHTS-002** for Temporary Protected Status, asylum access, and humanitarian reviewability.
- **Retain RIGHTS-003** because birthright citizenship presents a discrete constitutional and nationwide records question despite administrative overlap with RIGHTS-001.

Reroute removal due process, asylum, humanitarian status, and adjudicatory review to RIGHTS-002; domestic enforcement and detention operations to DOM; federalism to FED; and any independently supportable capacity defect to a later narrow record.

**Result:** 4 to 3. **Confidence:** high that the current scope fails the admission test; preserve all nonduplicative material during implementation.

Retain WAR-001. It is developed and Review Ready, and the area has no undeveloped inventory to consolidate.

**Result:** no change. **Confidence:** high.

## Recommended Implementation Sequence

After user approval, implement in reviewable batches rather than closing 121 records at once:

1. **Completed July 16, 2026 — high-confidence single-framework areas:** EMOL, FRB, HER, REC, RET, PAR, and CONG.
2. **High-confidence existing-home consolidations:** FUND, IMM, JUD, EMERG, DOM, and CIV.
3. **Boundary-sensitive areas:** CLASS, FACT, FED, OVS, PRESS, REG, and RIGHTS.
4. **No-change readback:** DOJ, ELEC, and WAR.

For each batch, confirm the receiving issue and vehicle; preserve all nonduplicative source leads and manifestations; update active lists and compact candidate-disposition tables; close but do not delete merged or rejected issues; remove inactive Project cards; synchronize the registry, Horizon log where applicable, Subject Index, topic routes, legislation index, research routes, and source inventory; read back Project rows; commit and push; then refresh and verify the dashboard. Scores and Runs do not change merely because of consolidation. A developed receiving proposal materially expanded by a batch requires a Change Audit and the appropriate Project marker.
