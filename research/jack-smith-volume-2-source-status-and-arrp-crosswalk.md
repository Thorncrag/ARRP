# Jack Smith Volume Two / Source Status and ARRP Crosswalk

## Purpose

This crosswalk records the source posture for Volume Two of Special Counsel Jack Smith's final report and maps the publicly available classified-documents materials to ARRP proposals. It does not treat indictments, press releases, or prosecutorial materials as proof of alleged facts.

Unlike Volume One, ARRP has not located a lawful public copy of Volume Two. The project should therefore treat Volume Two as a legally unavailable source unless and until DOJ, a court, or another lawful public authority releases it.

## Source Status

As of July 2, 2026, no lawful public Volume Two PDF has been retained in this repository.

The publicly released Volume One cover letter states that the final report has two volumes, that Volume Two addresses the Classified Documents Case, and that Volume Two should not be publicly released while the Nauta and De Oliveira case remained pending. See [SRC-0283](../inventory/sources.csv).

Public reporting and DOJ posture materials further indicate that Volume Two remained under court restrictions rather than publicly released. The Associated Press reported in January 2025 that DOJ planned to release the election-interference volume while keeping the classified-documents volume under wraps at least while co-defendant proceedings remained unresolved. See [SRC-0389](../inventory/sources.csv).

The underlying classified-documents criminal docket is *United States v. Trump*, No. 9:23-cr-80101 (S.D. Fla.). That docket should be distinguished from later Eleventh Circuit proceedings concerning report-release timing or access. The retained superseding indictment is the current lawful public substitute source for issue-spotting from the classified-documents case. See [SRC-0387](../inventory/sources.csv).

The later public docket posture should be treated as a source-status update, not as permission to use Volume Two. A Justia docket lead for Eleventh Circuit No. 25-13400, *In re American Oversight; In re Knight First Amendment Institute at Columbia University*, indicates that an earlier public-interest mandamus or timing challenge was dismissed as moot. See [SRC-0407](../inventory/sources.csv).

The current pertinent appellate lead appears to be Eleventh Circuit No. 26-10674, *Knight First Amendment Institute at Columbia University v. USA*. CourtListener identifies that appeal as filed March 3, 2026, from the S.D. Fla. criminal docket, No. 9:23-cr-80101; its docket entries indicate that No. 26-10674 was consolidated with No. 25-14507 for briefing and merits disposition, with last known filing activity on June 25, 2026. A later docket entry assigns the consolidated appeals to a tentative Miami calendar during the week of September 28, 2026. See [SRC-0408](../inventory/sources.csv). The project has not yet verified the official Eleventh Circuit docket sheet, orders, calendar notice, or dispositive filings, so publication-ready reliance should obtain the official appellate records. Unless a later lawful release occurs, the practical status remains unchanged: Volume Two is still not a lawful public source for ARRP.

The project should also distinguish an alleged unauthorized transmission from lawful public release. DOJ announced on May 20, 2026 that a former DOJ attorney had been indicted for allegedly transmitting a court-ordered sealed DOJ report outside DOJ under misleading file names. That supports the source-status conclusion that nonpublic copies may have moved outside ordinary channels, but it does not make Volume Two a lawful public source. See [SRC-0388](../inventory/sources.csv).

ARRP rule: do not download, retain, quote, summarize, or rely on any inadvertently disclosed, unauthorized, leaked, sealed, or otherwise nonpublic copy of Volume Two unless it is later lawfully unsealed or officially released. Use lawful public substitutes only.

## Summary Assessment

Volume Two itself cannot presently be used as a substantive source. The public classified-documents record nevertheless points to significant ARRP coverage and gap-check needs. The indictment and charging-related materials identify allegations, institutional-risk patterns, and source-development leads; they do not establish the alleged facts absent independent verification.

The strongest existing issue homes are:

- [DOJ-007](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-007.md), for independent investigation, protected counsel authority, evidence preservation, reporting limits, and report custody where ordinary DOJ control is conflicted.
- [JUD-009](../areas/JUD/issues/JUD-009.md), for appointer-president recusal and legitimacy-sensitive presidential-party litigation.
- [IMM-001](../areas/IMM/issues/IMM-001.md), for sitting-President prosecution barriers and delay-as-immunity concerns after reelection.

The strongest candidate-level source-development homes are:

- [A-07 Classification, Declassification, and National-Security Information](../areas/CLASS/README.md), especially CLASS-001 and CLASS-006.
- [A-13 Presidential Records, Archives, and Government Information](../areas/REC/README.md), especially REC-001.

No immediate new developed proposal is required solely because Volume Two is unavailable. The stronger conclusion is that A-07 and A-13 should be prioritized for development, and DOJ-007/JUD-009 should later be stress-tested against the classified-documents report-release dispute.

## Coverage Gaps and Follow-Up Reviews

### Highest-value follow-up audits

1. **A-07 development priority.** Develop CLASS-001 and CLASS-006 first, because the public record most directly exposes the gap between declassification rhetoric, lawful possession, secure storage, and accountability for unauthorized retention or disclosure.
2. **A-13 development priority.** Develop REC-001 as the records-side counterpart: NARA recovery authority, expedited litigation, custody controls, and presidential/personal record separation.
3. **DOJ-007 report-release stress test.** Test whether the independent-counsel proposal handles final reports in cases involving untried co-defendants, sealed or classified material, grand-jury restrictions, judicial orders, and congressional access.
4. **DOJ-007 appointment review.** Compare the proposal's appointment and funding mechanism against the classified-documents dismissal rationale and Appointments Clause objections.
5. **JUD-009 application review.** Use the classified-documents case as a source-development example for appointer-president recusal and public legitimacy in presidential-party litigation, without alleging actual bias.
6. **IMM-001 cross-check.** Confirm that sitting-President prosecution, tolling, preservation, and delayed accountability provisions work for classified-records cases as well as election-subversion cases.

## Source-Use Rules

When using this source cluster in issue pages, audits, or legislation notes:

- do not describe Volume Two as a source reviewed by ARRP unless it is later lawfully released and actually reviewed;
- do not use an unauthorized, leaked, sealed, or mistakenly disclosed copy of Volume Two;
- describe the superseding indictment as allegations in a charging document, not adjudicated findings;
- describe the DOJ May 20, 2026 press release as allegations in a separate indictment and source-status evidence, not proof of guilt;
- do not use an indictment, prosecutorial report, or charging press release to support the truth of an allegation unless the project verifies the specific cited evidence or corroborates the allegation through admitted records, judicial findings, official records, or other reliable sources;
- use indictments and prosecutorial materials to identify alleged fact patterns, procedural posture, source leads, and potential institutional flaws;
- pair report-release claims with official court orders or docket materials if they become accessible, including official Eleventh Circuit docket entries or orders for Nos. 25-13400, 25-14507, and 26-10674;
- preserve presumption-of-innocence language for untried charges, dismissed charges, uncharged persons, and any separate records-handling indictment;
- distinguish classified-information law, declassification, Presidential Records Act custody, National Archives recovery, obstruction, and special-counsel reporting as overlapping but separate institutional problems.

## Working Conclusion

Because Volume Two remains legally unavailable, the project should not attempt to summarize it. The lawful public record still has substantial project value: it confirms that ARRP needs stronger development in classification, records custody, National Archives enforcement, independent investigation, presidential-party litigation legitimacy, and sitting-President accountability.

The practical next step is not a new Volume Two factual narrative. It is targeted development of A-07 and A-13, followed by DOJ-007 and JUD-009 stress testing using only lawful public sources.
