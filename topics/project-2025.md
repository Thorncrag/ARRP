# Project 2025

## Overview

Project 2025 is a collection of policy, personnel, administrative, and institutional proposals associated with *Mandate for Leadership: The Conservative Promise*. ARRP considers those proposals only when they reveal, depend on, or could exploit a weakness in law, government structure, administration, enforcement, or available remedies. It does not treat Project 2025 as a single ARRP issue or regard every policy recommendation as an institutional failure.

This page connects commonly recognized Project 2025 themes to the ARRP proposals that address them. The linked proposal pages contain the detailed analysis and recommendations.

## Relevant Proposals

- **Public concern:** Presidential control of DOJ, investigations, or enforcement
  - **Applicable proposals:** [DOJ-003](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-003.md) and [DOJ-007](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-007.md)

- **Public concern:** Presidential refusal to carry out laws, maintain agencies or programs, or spend appropriated funds
  - **Applicable proposals:** [JUD-011](../areas/JUD/issues/JUD-011.md), [REG-001](../areas/REG/issues/REG-001.md), and [FUND-001](../areas/FUND/issues/FUND-001.md)

- **Public concern:** Political control of career civil servants and circumvention of appointment safeguards
  - **Applicable proposals:** CIV-001, [APPT-001](../areas/APPT/issues/APPT-001.md), and APPT-004

- **Public concern:** Emergency authority and domestic enforcement
  - **Applicable proposals:** EMERG-001, [EMERG-003](../areas/EMERG/issues/EMERG-003.md), DOM-001, and [WAR-001](../areas/WAR/issues/WAR-001.md)

- **Public concern:** Government information, science, and civil-rights administration
  - **Applicable proposals:** FACT-001 and [RIGHTS-001](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-001.md)

## How Concerns Map to Proposals

- **Public concern:** Schedule F-style reclassification or loyalty-based control of career officials
  - **Applicable proposals:** CIV-001
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses political reclassification of protected career positions.

- **Public concern:** Centralized Office of Management and Budget control, withholding funds, or refusal to spend appropriations
  - **Applicable proposals:** [FUND-001](../areas/FUND/issues/FUND-001.md) and [JUD-011](../areas/JUD/issues/JUD-011.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Address failures to carry out congressional mandates and appropriations.

- **Public concern:** Presidential control of DOJ or retaliatory enforcement
  - **Applicable proposals:** [DOJ-003](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-003.md) and [DOJ-007](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-007.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Address selective enforcement and independent investigation.

- **Public concern:** Immigration, asylum, detention, or large-scale interior enforcement
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-002](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-002.md) and DOM-001
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Separate access to humanitarian review from domestic deployment authority; broader immigration-policy choices remain outside ARRP absent a narrower institutional defect.

- **Public concern:** Abolition, closure, or disabling of agencies created by Congress
  - **Applicable proposals:** [REG-001](../areas/REG/issues/REG-001.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses agency continuity and presidential refusal to carry out enacted law.

- **Public concern:** Government-wide sex definitions, identity records, or civil-rights administration
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-001](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-001.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses civil-rights administration and identity records.

- **Public concern:** Suppression or distortion of government science, statistics, or public information
  - **Applicable proposals:** FACT-001
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses scientific alteration, dataset suppression, statistical interference, and suppression of unfavorable findings within one technical-integrity framework.

- **Public concern:** Presidential control over independent regulators or removal used to evade confirmation and succession safeguards
  - **Applicable proposals:** [REG-001](../areas/REG/issues/REG-001.md) and [APPT-001](../areas/APPT/issues/APPT-001.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** REG-001 addresses protected independence and functional disablement; APPT-001 addresses removal only when combined with vacancy, delegation, or succession practices that defeat confirmation or an appointment backstop.

- **Public concern:** Emergency or national-security authority used to bypass ordinary limits
  - **Applicable proposals:** EMERG-001, [EMERG-003](../areas/EMERG/issues/EMERG-003.md), DOM-001, and [WAR-001](../areas/WAR/issues/WAR-001.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Separates general emergencies, economic emergency powers, domestic force, and external force.

- **Public concern:** Government pressure on media, platforms, or protected public information
  - **Applicable proposals:** PRESS-001 and PRESS-006
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Separates denial of access from governmental retaliation.

- **Public concern:** Tax rates, benefit levels, energy production, or other ordinary policy choices
  - **Applicable proposals:** None
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** ARRP does not address those choices unless they reveal a separate institutional weakness.

## What ARRP Does and Does Not Address

ARRP addresses Project 2025 only where a proposal identifies or uses a separate institutional weakness. Disagreement over lawful tax, spending, benefit, energy, health, education, immigration, or social policy is not included merely because Project 2025 takes a position. ARRP includes a concern only when it involves a weakness in law, government structure, administration, enforcement, or available remedies rather than failure to enact a preferred policy outcome.
