# Federal Pressure on States and Cities

## Overview

The federal government may enforce federal law, attach lawful conditions to federal programs, investigate violations, and preempt conflicting State or local law within constitutional limits. The institutional problem is not federal supremacy itself. It is the possible use of funding, enforcement, waivers, investigations, force, or administrative control to compel unrelated policy changes, punish disfavored jurisdictions, or displace State functions without adequate authority and review.

This page separates those mechanisms so that disputes over sanctuary policies, election administration, investigations, grants, disaster aid, and federal deployments are not treated as one federalism question.

## Relevant Proposals

- **Public concern:** Funding, enforcement, investigation, or waiver pressure
  - **Applicable proposals:** FED-002 and [FED-003](../areas/FED/issues/FED-003.md)

- **Public concern:** Federal takeover, force deployment, or displacement of State functions
  - **Applicable proposals:** FED-004, DOM-001, and [DOM-005](../areas/DOM/issues/DOM-005.md)

- **Public concern:** Interference with State and local election administration
  - **Applicable proposals:** [ELEC-014](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-014.md)

- **Public concern:** Immigration cooperation, sanctuary policy, and rights-bearing process
  - **Applicable proposals:** [FED-003](../areas/FED/issues/FED-003.md) and [RIGHTS-002](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-002.md)

## How Concerns Map to Proposals

- **Public concern:** Conditioning disaster or infrastructure aid on an unrelated policy demand
  - **Applicable proposals:** [FED-003](../areas/FED/issues/FED-003.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses authority, germaneness, notice, and neutral eligibility.

- **Public concern:** Selective federal enforcement against a disfavored State or city
  - **Applicable proposals:** FED-002
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses comparators, documentation, review, and corrective safeguards.

- **Public concern:** Threatening grants to obtain sanctuary-policy or election-law changes
  - **Applicable proposals:** [FED-003](../areas/FED/issues/FED-003.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses statutory authority, germaneness, evidence, reporting, and expedited review.

- **Public concern:** Taking over or displacing an ordinary State function
  - **Applicable proposals:** FED-004
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses federal authority and limits on displacement of State administration.

- **Public concern:** Deploying federal forces over State objection
  - **Applicable proposals:** DOM-001
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses authority, findings, notice, duration, and review.

- **Public concern:** Federal executive interference with State election systems
  - **Applicable proposals:** [ELEC-014](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-014.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses limits on federal criminal investigations into state-administered elections.

- **Public concern:** Retaliatory federal investigations or audits
  - **Applicable proposals:** FED-002
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses neutral predicates, documentation, comparators, and review.

- **Public concern:** Coercive federal waivers or approvals
  - **Applicable proposals:** [FED-003](../areas/FED/issues/FED-003.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses authority, germaneness, notice, evidence, and review.

- **Public concern:** Federal control of evidence needed for a State critical-incident investigation
  - **Applicable proposals:** [DOM-005](../areas/DOM/issues/DOM-005.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses preservation and secure State access without changing immunity or prosecution authority.

## What ARRP Does and Does Not Address

ARRP addresses coercion, retaliation, displacement, evidence access, and review within the federal system. It does not prevent lawful federal preemption, enforcement of federal law, relevant funding conditions, or emergency assistance requirements. It also does not treat States or cities as immune from federal investigation merely because their officials oppose federal policy.
