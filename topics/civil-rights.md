# Civil Rights

## Overview

ARRP’s civil-rights work focuses on whether government institutions administer rights through clear authority, stable records, fair procedures, reviewable decisions, neutral enforcement, and effective remedies. Current proposals address civil-rights definitions and enforcement, identity and citizenship records, humanitarian protection, immigration due process, voting access, and representative fairness. The primary institutional map is [Civil Rights Administration and Identity Records](../areas/RIGHTS/README.md).

This page connects common public concerns to the proposals that address them. It does not decide every disputed question of civil-rights policy or treat every unequal outcome as proof of a separate institutional failure. The linked proposal pages contain the detailed legal analysis and recommendations.

## Relevant Proposals

- **Public concern:** Civil-rights administration and identity or citizenship records
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-001](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-001.md) and [RIGHTS-003](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-003.md)

- **Public concern:** Humanitarian protection and immigration due process
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-002](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-002.md)

- **Public concern:** Voting access and representative fairness
  - **Applicable proposals:** [ELEC-010](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-010.md) and [ELEC-011](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-011.md)

## How Concerns Map to Proposals

- **Public concern:** Government-wide civil-rights definitions, identity records, grant conditions, privacy, or detention classification
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-001](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-001.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses stable authority, records, privacy, procedures, and review across federal civil-rights administration.

- **Public concern:** Temporary Protected Status, asylum access, or review of humanitarian decisions
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-002](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-002.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses reviewable humanitarian-status decisions and access to statutory protection.

- **Public concern:** Birthright citizenship, citizenship recognition, or federal identity documents
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-003](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-003.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses statutory recognition, records continuity, benefits, and judicial review.

- **Public concern:** Removal proceedings, detention process, or humanitarian status
  - **Applicable proposals:** [RIGHTS-002](../areas/RIGHTS/issues/RIGHTS-002.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses statutory humanitarian protection and review; broader immigration-policy choices remain outside ARRP absent a narrower institutional defect.

- **Public concern:** Mail voting, voter access, or other barriers to casting and counting votes
  - **Applicable proposals:** [ELEC-010](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-010.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses reliable and accessible voting procedures without selecting partisan outcomes.

- **Public concern:** Redistricting, gerrymandering, vote dilution, or representative fairness
  - **Applicable proposals:** [ELEC-011](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-011.md)
  - **How ARRP addresses it:** Addresses neutral districting safeguards, representation, and remedies for unfair district structures.

## Related Ideas Not Included

- **Idea:** [HOR-020 — District of Columbia statehood](https://github.com/Thorncrag/ARRP/issues/18)
  - **Why it is not included:** Statehood is a substantive political-status choice. ARRP may still address a separate, status-neutral rights, administration, due-process, home-rule, or representation defect.

- **Idea:** [HOR-021 — Puerto Rico political status](https://github.com/Thorncrag/ARRP/issues/19)
  - **Why it is not included:** Choosing statehood, independence, free association, territorial status, or another final arrangement is a substantive political decision. ARRP may still address a separate, status-neutral rights or administrative defect.

## What ARRP Does and Does Not Address

ARRP addresses weaknesses in the administration, enforcement, review, and protection of civil rights. It may propose status-neutral safeguards for due process, anti-discrimination, identity and citizenship records, humanitarian protection, voting, representation, benefits, and access to adjudication. It does not select the District of Columbia’s or Puerto Rico’s final political status, resolve every disputed substantive rights question, or replace ordinary constitutional and democratic decision-making with a preferred policy outcome.
