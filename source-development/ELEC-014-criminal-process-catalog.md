# ELEC-014 Criminal-Process and State Election Records Catalog

This catalog supports [ELEC-014](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-014.md). It records known federal criminal probes, warrants, subpoenas, prosecution-threat communications, and state-election-record demands relevant to election-sensitive federal criminal process.

The catalog is factual and source-development oriented. It does not decide motive, partisan purpose, legality, or legitimacy. Those judgments require the next-stage T2 remedy-selection and legitimacy assessment, using primary records wherever available.

## Scope Rules

**Included as ELEC-014 core material:** federal criminal investigations, search warrants, grand-jury subpoenas, federal law-enforcement interviews, device or document seizures, criminal subpoenas, criminal prosecution threats, and federal investigative data demands directed at state-administered elections, election officials, voter-registration activity, voting-rights organizations, ballots, voter data, voting systems, or election records.

**Included as adjacent state-record-demand material:** DOJ Civil Rights Division, Voting Section, or other federal requests, letters, lawsuits, or information-sharing proposals seeking state voter rolls, election records, voter identifiers, election-worker records, or election-administration information where the record does not yet show a criminal subpoena, warrant, grand jury, or prosecution threat.

**Not treated as proof of misuse:** the existence of a search, subpoena, investigation, lawsuit, data request, or prosecution warning. This catalog preserves the facts needed to later assess predicate, timing, necessity, neutrality, scope, minimization, and legitimacy.

## Verification Needs Before Legitimacy Assessment

1. Obtain primary warrant materials, returns, inventories, and docket entries for the Ohio and Georgia searches.
2. Obtain the Fulton County grand-jury subpoena, motion to quash, transcript if available, and Judge William Ray's order.
3. Verify the reported Arizona grand-jury subpoena from a reliable accessible source or primary records.
4. Build a state-by-state voter-record-demand table from DOJ letters, complaints, court orders, and state responses rather than relying only on AP aggregate counts.
5. Obtain the July 2026 DOJ 50-state letter and code whether it is a legal reminder, investigative demand, prosecution warning, or predicate for later enforcement.
6. Separate legitimate federal enforcement categories from potentially partisan or election-distorting categories before drafting any remedy.
