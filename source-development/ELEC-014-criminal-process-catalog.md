# ELEC-014 Criminal-Process and State Election Records Catalog

This catalog supports [ELEC-014](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-014.md). It records known federal criminal probes, warrants, grand-jury subpoenas, criminal subpoenas, prosecution-threat communications, and criminal-investigative state-election-record demands relevant to election-sensitive federal criminal process.

The catalog is factual and source-development oriented. It does not decide motive, partisan purpose, legality, or legitimacy. Those judgments require the next-stage T2 remedy-selection and legitimacy assessment, using primary records wherever available.

## Scope Rules

**Included as ELEC-014 core material:** federal criminal investigations, search warrants, grand-jury subpoenas, federal law-enforcement interviews, device or document seizures, criminal subpoenas, criminal prosecution threats, and federal investigative data demands directed at state-administered elections, election officials, voter-registration activity, voting-rights organizations, ballots, voter data, voting systems, or election records.

**Excluded from ELEC-014 core scope:** civil, administrative, or Voting Section voter-roll requests, voter-data lawsuits, information-sharing proposals, and noncriminal state-election-record demands. Those may be tracked as boundary material because they can become source leads or belong in ELEC-001/ELEC-010, but ELEC-014 should treat them as in scope only if the record shows a warrant, grand jury, criminal subpoena, federal criminal investigation, Criminal Division investigative channel, or criminal prosecution threat.

**Not treated as proof of misuse:** the existence of a search, subpoena, investigation, lawsuit, data request, or prosecution warning. This catalog preserves the facts needed to later assess predicate, timing, necessity, neutrality, scope, minimization, and legitimacy.

## Boundary Catalog: Noncriminal State Election Record Demands

These items are not ELEC-014 core manifestations. They are retained only to preserve the criminal/civil boundary, identify source leads that might later reveal criminal process, and route noncriminal federal election-control or voter-data issues to ELEC-001, ELEC-010, or another proper home. They should not be used as ELEC-014 evidence unless a warrant, grand jury, criminal subpoena, prosecution threat, or criminal-investigative channel is independently verified.

## Verification Needs Before Legitimacy Assessment

1. For every criminal-process catalog entry, identify the exact alleged statute or offense theory from the warrant, application, affidavit, subpoena, DOJ letter, court order, or official explanation.
2. Obtain primary warrant materials, returns, inventories, and docket entries for the Ohio and Georgia searches.
3. Obtain the Fulton County grand-jury subpoena, motion to quash, transcript if available, and Judge William Ray's order.
4. Verify the reported Arizona grand-jury subpoena from a reliable accessible source or primary records.
5. Move purely civil or administrative voter-data litigation out of ELEC-014 analysis unless a criminal-process hook is verified.
6. Obtain the July 2026 DOJ 50-state letter and code the exact statutory citations and whether it is a legal reminder, investigative demand, prosecution warning, or predicate for later enforcement.
7. Separate legitimate federal enforcement categories from potentially partisan or election-distorting categories before drafting any remedy.
