# Jack Smith Volume One / ARRP Crosswalk

## Purpose

This crosswalk maps Volume One of Special Counsel Jack Smith's final report to ARRP proposals. The goal is to ensure that the report's institutional concerns, alleged fact patterns, procedural posture, and source leads are either already addressed by an existing proposal, routed to the correct issue for later source development, or flagged as a potential gap.

The report should be used with care. It is a prosecutor's final report and source-development record, not an adjudicated judicial finding after trial. It may identify institutional flaws and point to evidence, but it should not be used to support the truth of an allegation unless the project verifies the specific cited evidence or corroborates the claim through admitted records, judicial findings, official records, or other reliable sources. It should be paired with docket orders, Supreme Court opinions, the January 6 Select Committee report, state records, final court decisions, and other independently verifiable materials before any publication-ready factual claim relies on it.

## Summary Assessment

The report is most directly relevant to [ELEC-008](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-008.md), because its central institutional concern is that the post-2020 episode exposed a coordinated, multi-channel election-subversion risk rather than a single isolated vulnerability.

The report is not limited to ELEC-008, however. Its component assertions and institutional lessons also identify or test the following issue homes:

- [ELEC-001](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-001.md) for federal executive pressure on state election administration.
- [ELEC-002](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-002.md) for certification and canvassing vulnerability.
- [ELEC-003](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-003.md) for threats, intimidation, and official/family targeting.
- [ELEC-004](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-004.md) for abusive litigation or investigatory mechanisms.
- [ELEC-005](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-005.md) for false or unauthorized presidential result-transmission documents.
- [ELEC-006](../areas/ELEC/issues/ELEC-006.md) for peaceful-transfer and transition-continuity vulnerabilities.
- [DOJ-002](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-002.md) for White House or political interference in particular criminal matters.
- [DOJ-007](../areas/DOJ/issues/DOJ-007.md) for independent investigation, evidence preservation, and reporting where ordinary DOJ control is conflicted.
- [IMM-001](../areas/IMM/issues/IMM-001.md) for presidential criminal immunity, official-act evidence, and sitting-President prosecution constraints.

No immediate new developed issue is required solely because of this report. The strongest follow-up candidates are an overlap review of the remaining immunity candidates in [A-16](../areas/IMM/README.md), especially IMM-003, IMM-005, and IMM-006, and a later [A-15](../areas/CONG/README.md) review of congressional-count, contempt, and oversight-collapse issues.

## Coverage Gaps and Follow-Up Reviews

### No immediate new proposal required

The report does not appear to require an entirely new developed proposal at this stage. ARRP already has issue homes for the major institutional defects identified in the report.

### Highest-value follow-up audits

1. **ELEC-008 source-development refresh.** Use the report as a central source lead for the coordinated-scheme theory, but pair it with the superseding indictment, final dismissal order, *Fischer*, *Trump v. United States*, and any official docket materials.
2. **ELEC-005 contingent-document review.** Stress-test the safe harbor for lawful contingent elector activity against the report's description of how contingent framing allegedly became false-document leverage.
3. **DOJ-002 / DOJ-007 cross-audit.** Use the DOJ-pressure findings to test whether White House-DOJ routing, logging, independent investigation, evidence preservation, and reporting safeguards are complete.
4. **IMM-001 follow-up.** Treat the report as an application record showing how official-act immunity affected a real prosecution, while keeping *Trump v. United States* as the controlling legal source.
5. **A-16 candidate overlap review.** Decide whether IMM-003, IMM-005, and IMM-006 should remain separate issues or be collapsed into IMM-001/DOJ-007 annotations.
6. **A-15 later review.** Decide whether congressional-count pressure and partisan collapse of oversight are adequately handled by ELEC-008, ECRA discussion, and DOJ-007, or whether a congressional-accountability issue should be developed.

## Source-Use Rules

When using this report in issue pages, audits, or legislation notes:

- describe it as a Special Counsel report, prosecution assessment, or source-development lead;
- do not describe its factual narrative as a final judicial finding or as independently verified fact;
- do not use the report to support the truth of an allegation unless the project verifies the specific cited evidence or corroborates the allegation through admitted records, judicial findings, official records, or other reliable sources;
- use it freely for identifying institutional flaws, procedural posture, source leads, and remedy stress tests, with proper qualification;
- distinguish original indictment allegations, superseding indictment allegations, immune conduct, non-immune conduct, and dismissed-case posture;
- pair immunity propositions with the Supreme Court opinion in *Trump v. United States*;
- pair obstruction propositions with *Fischer* and applicable docket materials;
- pair false-elector propositions with NARA, congressional, state, or court records where possible;
- pair election-worker threat propositions with the January 6 Select Committee report or specific prosecution records where possible;
- preserve presumption-of-innocence language for untried charges and uncharged persons.

## Working Conclusion

The report strengthens the existing ARRP architecture rather than displacing it. Its main value is not creating a new issue, but confirming that the project should treat the 2020 election-subversion episode as a multi-system institutional failure: election administration, certification, false result documents, abusive process, DOJ independence, congressional count procedures, transition continuity, presidential immunity, and impeachment/prosecution boundaries all interacted.

ARRP's current proposal set substantially covers those interactions, but the report should trigger targeted follow-up audits and source refreshes for ELEC-008, ELEC-005, DOJ-002, DOJ-007, and IMM-001.
